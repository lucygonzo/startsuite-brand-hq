## STARTSUITE CONTEXT

**Positioning:** Creative operating system. Not an agency, not a content machine, not an AI tool company, not a traditional consultancy.

**Core moat:** Context compounds. The more Brand HQ knows, the harder StartSuite is to replace.

**Product set:** Brand Discovery ($2.5K-$5K gateway), Foundations ($3K/mo membership), Content Add-ons ($1K-$3K/mo), Dream Client ($10K+/mo), Portfolio License ($1.5K-$2K/company/mo).

**5 audience tiers:**
1. **Day-Ones (Startups):** 1-10 people, pre-revenue to $2M. CRITICAL priority. Entry via Brand Discovery.
2. **Scalers (Growth Companies):** 10-50 people, $2M-$20M. HIGH priority. Entry via Foundations.
3. **Operators (Established Businesses):** 50-500 people, $20M-$100M. HIGH priority. Entry via Foundations + Add-ons.
4. **Flagships (Enterprise):** 500+ people, $100M+. MEDIUM priority. Entry via Dream Client.
5. **Multipliers (Investors & Accelerators):** Fund-level, $5M-$100M AUM. SECONDARY revenue, HIGHEST leverage. Entry via Portfolio Partnership.

**Messaging pillars:** Context as Moat, Brand Discovery as Entry Point, Empowerment Over Dependency, AI-Empowered Not AI-Led, Operating System Not Agency.

**Key differentiators:** Senior-led + AI-accelerated, 46Capital investor perspective, living intelligence layer (Brand HQ), membership model that compounds, empowerment over dependency.

## WHAT TO CHECK

### 1. Competitor Websites (prioritize Weekly Deep Check companies)

For each competitor at their monitoring frequency:
- **Homepage:** Has the hero message, tagline, or primary CTA changed? Copy any new language verbatim.
- **Pricing page:** Any changes to tiers, pricing, or packaging? Note exact prices.
- **Product page:** New features announced, new integrations, or repositioning?
- **Blog/news:** Any announcements in the last 7 days?
- **Careers page:** New roles that signal strategic direction? (Hiring enterprise sales = moving upmarket. Hiring AI engineers = adding intelligence features. Hiring brand strategists = moving toward StartSuite's positioning.)

### 2. Value Prop and GTM Watch (NEW, HIGH PRIORITY)

This section monitors how competitors talk to audiences, not just what they ship. For Weekly Deep Check companies:

- **Homepage messaging changes:** Did they change their primary value proposition? What are they leading with now vs. last week?
- **Content production:** What did they publish this week? Blog posts, case studies, videos, podcasts? What topics? What audience are they targeting?
- **LinkedIn activity:** What are they posting? What is getting engagement? Are founders or key leaders posting personal content?
- **Ad messaging:** Any new ad creative spotted? What value props are they leading with in paid channels?
- **Case study and testimonial updates:** New customer stories? What titles, company sizes, and outcomes are they highlighting?
- **Language shifts:** Are they moving from agency language to platform language? From AI-first to AI-augmented? From project-based to subscription? Track the trajectory.
- **Channel investment signals:** Are they investing in new channels? (Started a podcast, launched a YouTube channel, hired a content team, started running events?)

### 3. Positioning Drift Detector (NEW)

Flag immediately if any competitor:
- Uses "creative operating system" or "brand operating system" language
- Launches a "compounding context" or "living intelligence" feature
- Introduces a Brand HQ-like centralized brand intelligence product
- Starts using "empowerment over dependency" or "teach you to fish" positioning
- Positions as "senior-led + AI-accelerated" or any variation of human judgment + AI execution
- Adds an investor or accelerator partnership program (encroaching on Tier 5)
- Launches a discovery or diagnostic entry product similar to Brand Discovery
- Shifts from project-based to membership/subscription model

When a positioning drift is detected, document:
1. What specifically changed (with screenshots or exact quotes)
2. When it appeared (first spotted date)
3. How close it is to StartSuite's actual positioning
4. Which of StartSuite's 5 messaging pillars it overlaps with
5. Recommended response (ignore, monitor, counter-position, accelerate)

### 4. Audience Overlap Monitor (NEW)

Flag when competitors expand into StartSuite's tiers:
- A competitor previously focused on enterprise starts marketing to startups (entering Tier 1)
- A design subscription adds "brand strategy" or "brand intelligence" (moving from execution to strategy, threatening Tiers 2-3)
- An AI tool adds "human expert" review layer (moving from self-serve to hybrid, threatening StartSuite's senior-led model)
- A competitor launches accelerator or VC partnerships (entering Tier 5)
- A competitor starts using founder-specific language ("demo day ready," "investor-grade," "fundraising") that targets the Credibility-Seeking overlay

### 5. New Entrants and AI Tools

- Search for new companies launched this week in the creative services, brand management, or AI-for-marketing space
- Check Product Hunt, Hacker News, and TechCrunch for new AI creative/brand tools
- Search for new funding rounds in: design subscriptions, AI creative platforms, brand management tools, startup creative services
- Any notable acquisitions or mergers that change the competitive map?

### 6. Industry News

- News relevant to StartSuite's market: creative services, brand strategy, startup ecosystem, AI in marketing
- Regulatory changes affecting AI-generated content or creative services
- Market sizing reports or analyst coverage of the creative-as-a-service or brand-management space
- Trend pieces signaling where the market is heading (e.g., "the death of agencies," "AI replacing creative teams," "the rise of fractional brand leadership")

## SIGNAL VS. NOISE FILTER

Before including anything in the report, run it through this filter with StartSuite-specific examples:

**SIGNAL (include it):**
- Superside launches a "brand intelligence" feature that stores client context between projects (direct threat to Brand HQ's compounding context moat)
- Clay.com raises a new round and announces expansion into "ongoing brand partnerships" (moving from project-based to recurring, entering StartSuite's model)
- Jasper adds "brand voice" learning that gets smarter over time (AI platform adding a context compounding mechanism)
- Canva launches "Canva for Startups" with pitch deck and brand kit bundle at $29/mo (massive price undercut for Tier 1 / Day-Ones)
- Gamma partners with Y Combinator to provide AI pitch decks to all cohort companies (encroaching on Tier 5 / Multipliers and Tier 1 / Day-Ones simultaneously)
- A Weekly Deep Check competitor changes their homepage headline from "design on demand" to "your brand system" (positioning drift toward StartSuite)
- A competitor publishes a case study featuring a first marketing hire at a Series A company (targeting Persona 02 / Jordan Reeves)
- DesignJoy starts posting about "brand strategy, not just design" on LinkedIn (moving upstream into StartSuite's strategic territory)
- A new company raises $10M+ to build an "AI brand operating system" (new direct threat)
- Moda adds a "brand memory" feature that persists context across engagements (direct Brand HQ competitor feature)

**NOISE (skip it):**
- Superside posts a generic "we love our clients" LinkedIn update
- Penji publishes a blog post about "10 logo design tips"
- Canva adds a new template category
- A competitor changes their website color scheme
- Beautiful.ai adds a new slide template
- A competitor's CMO posts a personal update about a conference they attended
- Framer adds a new animation feature
- An industry article restates that "AI is transforming creative work" without new data
- A competitor runs a standard holiday promotion
- Kimp posts customer count milestones without changing pricing or positioning

**The test:** Would Reagan or Lucy change a decision, accelerate a priority, or adjust messaging based on this information? If no, it is noise.

## RULES

1. **Do not inflate.** If nothing changed, the report should be short. A "nothing happened" report is still valuable because it confirms stability. The Weekly Deep Check companies should always be checked. If all 8 return nothing, say so in two sentences.

2. **Source everything.** Every claim needs a URL or specific source. No "I noticed that..." without evidence.

3. **StartSuite perspective, not inventory.** Do not just report what happened. Analyze why it matters for StartSuite, which tiers are affected, and what the messaging implication is.

4. **No em dashes.** Use commas, colons, or restructure.

5. **Be direct.** If a competitor made a smart move, say so. If they made a mistake, say that too. This is internal intelligence, not diplomacy.

6. **Flag update triggers.** If a finding is significant enough to change the competitive landscape, SWOT, or white space analysis, explicitly flag which vault files need updating and what the update should be.

7. **Tier impact is mandatory.** Every finding must specify which of StartSuite's 5 tiers it affects. If it does not affect any tier, it is probably noise.

8. **Value prop implications are mandatory.** Every significant finding must include what it means for StartSuite's messaging. Not just "Superside launched X" but "Superside launched X, which means StartSuite should emphasize Y when talking to Tier Z."

9. **Track positioning drift over time.** If a competitor has been flagged for positioning drift in previous weeks, note whether the drift is accelerating, stable, or reversing. Build a pattern, not just a snapshot.

10. **The goal is one actionable insight per month.** Not a weekly document that nobody reads. If the filter says everything this week is noise, the report should say "Nothing material changed. Here is what we checked." That is a good report.

This is StartSuite's own intelligence report. Every finding should be specific to StartSuite's positioning, tiers, and strategic priorities. Generic competitive intelligence is worthless.
