# StartSuite: What Not to Say

> Last updated: March 26, 2026
> Source: March 2026 board meeting pivot context, `brandData.ts` identity and verbal identity sections

---

## The Core Rule

StartSuite is a **creative operating system**. Every word choice must reinforce this positioning. The March 2026 pivot explicitly retired the old "creative infrastructure" language and redefined what StartSuite is and is not.

---

## We Are NOT an Agency

| Don't Say | Say Instead |
|-----------|-------------|
| "Our agency" | "Our creative operating system" |
| "Agency services" | "Creative operating system services" |
| "Full-service agency" | "Full-service creative operating system" |
| "We're a creative agency for startups" | "We're a creative operating system for founders and growing companies" |
| "Hire us as your agency" | "Run your brand on StartSuite" |

**Why:** The word "agency" triggers expectations of project-based work, junior handoffs, slow timelines, and bloated retainers. StartSuite is a system, not a team for hire.

---

## We Are NOT a Content Machine

| Don't Say | Say Instead |
|-----------|-------------|
| "We create content" | "We build brand intelligence" |
| "Content production" | "Strategic context management" |
| "We'll handle your social media" | "We empower your team with brand tools and strategic context" |
| "Deliverables" (as the primary value prop) | "Living intelligence layer" |

**Why:** Content is an output. StartSuite's value is the compounding intelligence layer. Leading with content commoditizes the offering.

---

## We Are NOT an AI Tool Company

| Don't Say | Say Instead |
|-----------|-------------|
| "AI-powered brand tool" | "AI-empowered creative operating system" |
| "Our AI creates your brand" | "Senior creative judgment leads. AI accelerates execution." |
| "AI-first" | "AI-empowered, not AI-led" |
| "Built on AI" | "Powered by senior creative talent who use AI to accelerate" |
| "Our AI technology" | "Our creative operating system" |

**Why:** Positioning as an AI company invites comparison to Jasper, Writer, Copy.ai, and Canva AI. Those tools have no context, no judgment, and no taste. StartSuite competes on a different axis entirely. AI is the engine, not the brand.

---

## We Are NOT a Traditional Consultancy

| Don't Say | Say Instead |
|-----------|-------------|
| "Brand consulting" | "Creative operating system" |
| "Strategic consulting engagement" | "Brand Discovery" |
| "Our consultants" | "Our team" or "Reagan and Lucy" |
| "Assessment and recommendations" | "Brand Discovery produces a living Brand HQ" |

**Why:** Consultancies deliver strategy decks that sit on a shelf. StartSuite delivers a living system that compounds over time.

## Retired Language (Pre-March 2026 Pivot)

These terms were used before the March 2026 pivot and must not appear in any new materials:

| Retired Term | Current Term |
|--------------|-------------|
| "Creative infrastructure" | "Creative operating system" |
| "Brand infrastructure" | "Brand intelligence layer" |
| "Creative partner" (as primary descriptor) | "Creative operating system" |
| "Brand management service" | "Living intelligence layer" |

---

## Other Terms to Avoid

- **"Customers"** or **"clients"** in external copy — use "founders", "teams", or "companies"
- **"Best"** or **"leading"** without proof — superlatives require evidence
- **"Excited to announce"** — just announce it
- **"Comprehensive solution"** — vague and corporate
- **"End-to-end"** — overused and meaningless
- **"Leverage"** (as a verb) — use "use" or "build on"
- **"Synergy"** — never
- **"Disrupt"** — overused in startup contexts
- **"World-class"** — prove it or don't say it

---

## The Positioning Litmus Test

Before publishing any copy, ask:

1. Does this sound like it could come from any agency? If yes, rewrite.
2. Does this reinforce the "creative operating system" positioning? If no, rewrite.
3. Does this speak to founders as equals? If it sounds like a vendor pitch, rewrite.
4. Does this reference compounding intelligence or context as moat? If the copy could describe a one-time project, rewrite.
5. Would an expert share this? If it sounds like generic marketing, kill it.
