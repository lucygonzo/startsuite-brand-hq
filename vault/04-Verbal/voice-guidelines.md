# StartSuite Voice Guidelines

> Last updated: March 26, 2026
> Source: `brandData.ts` — `verbalIdentity` (keyTakeaway, toneAttributes, voiceDontList)

---

## Key Takeaway

The StartSuite voice is confident, direct, and strategic. It speaks to founders and growing companies as equals — acknowledging the pressures they face and offering a system, not just services. The tone is authoritative but never arrogant, and it uses contrast and data to make its points land.

---

## Tone Attributes

### Confident
Makes declarative statements. Does not hedge. Does not over-explain.

- **Do:** "Agencies are too slow. Freelancers are too fragmented. AI-only tools have no taste. We built a better model."
- **Don't:** "We think we might be able to offer a potentially better alternative to some of the existing options in the market."

### Direct
Front-loads the point. No preamble. No throat-clearing.

- **Do:** "Your brand should be as smart as your product."
- **Don't:** "In today's fast-paced startup environment, it can be challenging to ensure that your brand narrative keeps pace with your company's growth trajectory."

### Strategic
Speaks to outcomes, not just outputs. Connects creative to business results.

- **Do:** "Context is the moat. The more Brand HQ knows, the harder it is to replace."
- **Don't:** "We make beautiful logos and websites for your company."

### Founder-Peer
Speaks to founders and teams as equals — not as a vendor, not as a manager.

- **Do:** "We learn your story first. Then we build the system."
- **Don't:** "Our team of experts will assess your brand needs and develop a comprehensive solution."

### Data-Backed
Uses numbers and research to support claims — but never at the expense of readability.

- **Do:** "94% of first impressions are design-driven. You have 0.05 seconds."
- **Don't:** "Research suggests that visual elements may play a role in how people perceive your brand."

---

## Voice Don'ts

1. No em dashes in body copy — use periods or restructure the sentence
2. No passive voice — "we build" not "is built by us"
3. No jargon without immediate plain-language follow-up
4. No superlatives without proof — "best" requires evidence
5. No exclamation points in professional copy — confidence doesn't need punctuation to shout
6. No "we're excited to announce" — just announce it
