## Quick-Reference Messaging Angles by Tier

### Tier 1: Startups
- **Lead with:** Speed and credibility. "Look like you've raised 5x what you have. In 48 hours."
- **Avoid:** "Brand strategy" language. They don't think they need strategy. They think they need a logo and a deck.
- **Entry:** Brand Discovery call. Low commitment, high value.
- **Key proof:** Before/after transformations. Founder testimonials. Speed stats.

### Tier 2: Growth Companies
- **Lead with:** System and relief. "The brand system you don't have time to build yourself."
- **Avoid:** Agency language. Many have had bad freelancer/agency experiences.
- **Entry:** Foundations directly. They have the budget and the pain.
- **Key proof:** First-marketing-hire case studies. Time savings quantified.

### Tier 3: Established Businesses
- **Lead with:** Defense and intelligence. "The brand intelligence that defends your decisions and unifies your divisions."
- **Avoid:** Creative jargon. These buyers want data, ROI, and accountability.
- **Entry:** Foundations + competitive intelligence add-on.
- **Key proof:** Multi-division case studies. Competitive intelligence samples. Decision audit log demo.

### Tier 4: Enterprise
- **Lead with:** Strategic augmentation. "Make your team of 12 perform like 20."
- **Avoid:** Hand-holding language, startup-focused messaging, "AI-powered" without substance.
- **Entry:** Strategic consultation, then pilot on one product line or channel.
- **Key proof:** Enterprise brand leader testimonials. Competitive intelligence depth. The quality of the first conversation.

### Tier 5: Investors & Accelerators
- **Lead with:** Portfolio ROI. "Your portfolio's brand is your fund's brand."
- **Avoid:** Individual company pitches. They need the portfolio story.
- **Entry:** Portfolio partnership with pilot on 3 companies.
- **Key proof:** Fund-level before/after. Demo day quality improvement. The QuickBooks comparison.

## The Single Biggest Audience Insight

> "Nobody ever comes and says, 'I don't have a brand voice.' They say, 'I need posts.'" -- Lucy

**The implication:** StartSuite must market to symptoms, not root causes. Every tier, every persona, every overlay leads with the pain they feel (can't make content, can't defend decisions, look amateur, tools don't work) and surfaces the root cause (brand infrastructure gap) through the conversation, not the ad.

The sale happens when the buyer has what Lucy calls the "spark in the eye" moment -- when they realize the thing they've been struggling with has a name (brand infrastructure) and a solution (StartSuite).
