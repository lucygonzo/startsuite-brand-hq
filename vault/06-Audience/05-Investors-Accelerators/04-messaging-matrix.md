## Sample Copy

### LinkedIn Post (for StartSuite's Account)

**Hook:** Every VC says they add value beyond the check. Here's what that actually looks like for brand.

**Body:** We partnered with a seed fund to deploy brand infrastructure across their entire portfolio. Before: 15 companies with 15 different levels of brand quality. Some looked Series B-ready. Some looked like they were built in Google Slides over a weekend. After: every company has a Brand HQ -- a living brand operating system with positioning, competitive intelligence, voice guidelines, and a visual system that actually works. The cost per company was less than one month of freelance design work. Demo day went from "mixed bag" to "they all look like they know what they're doing." When your portfolio companies look institutional, your fund looks institutional.

**CTA:** If you manage a portfolio and want to see what this looks like, DM me.

### Email to Accelerator Program Director

**Subject:** Brand infrastructure for [Program Name] cohorts

**Body:**

Hi [Name],

Quick question: how many companies in your current cohort have a consistent brand system -- logo, positioning, voice, competitive landscape, all documented in one place?

If the answer is "not many," you're not alone. Most accelerator companies graduate with a product and a pitch deck but no brand infrastructure. Then they spend months scrambling to look professional enough for investors.

We built a brand operating system that deploys in days, not months. For accelerator cohorts, we offer a brand module: a 2-hour workshop + Brand Discovery for every company + 3 months of Foundations (our brand intelligence platform) at cohort pricing.

Companies graduate with a live Brand HQ -- not a static PDF. It includes their positioning, competitive landscape, brand voice, visual system, and a decision audit log. It's the thing they pull up when an investor asks "tell me about your brand" -- and it makes your program look like it builds companies, not just funds them.

Would it be worth a 20-minute call to see if this fits your next cohort?
