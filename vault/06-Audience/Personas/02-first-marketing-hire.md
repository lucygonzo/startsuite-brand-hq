# Persona Profile: The First Marketing Hire

**Persona:** 02 -- First Marketing Hire
**Primary Tier:** Tier 2 (Growth Stage)
**Secondary Tier:** Tier 1 (late Startups)
**Overlays:** Time-Starved (extreme), AI-Overwhelmed (strong), Credibility-Seeking (moderate)

---

## Meet Jordan Reeves

**Age:** 29
**Title:** Head of Marketing, Gridline (B2B SaaS for construction project management)
**Company stage:** Series A. Raised $6M four months ago. 22 employees and hiring fast.
**Team:** Just Jordan. The company had zero marketing people before him. The CEO did everything -- poorly.
**Location:** Nashville, TN. Moved from Atlanta for the role.
**Education:** UGA marketing degree. Worked at HubSpot for 2 years (customer marketing), then at a Series B fintech startup for 18 months before it ran out of money.
**Personal:** Lives with his girlfriend in Germantown. Plays pickup basketball on Wednesdays. Hasn't played in 3 weeks because he's "drowning."

## Day in the Life

**6:30 AM** -- Alarm. Immediately opens Slack on his phone. 8 messages from the CEO who was "brainstorming at midnight." Half of them are links to competitor websites with notes like "why don't we look like this?" and "can you make something like this by Thursday?"

**7:15 AM** -- Coffee. Opens his laptop at the kitchen table. Creates a to-do list for the day. It has 14 items. He will complete 3 of them. The rest will roll to tomorrow's list, which already has 11 items from yesterday.

**7:45 AM** -- Opens the company's Google Drive. Tries to find the brand assets. There are 4 folders that might contain them: "Marketing," "Brand Stuff," "Lucy's Designs" (Lucy was an intern who left 8 months ago), and "FINAL LOGOS DO NOT EDIT." He opens each one. Combined, they contain 127 files with names like "logo-v4-USE-THIS.png," "gridline_blue_option2.ai," and "homepage-hero-old-DONT-USE.jpg." He has no idea which files are current. The company's logo exists in 6 slightly different versions.

**8:30 AM** -- Standup with the engineering team. Jordan is supposed to present the marketing plan for Q2. He doesn't have a marketing plan for Q2. He has a spreadsheet with "content ideas" and a vague sense that they need to "do more on LinkedIn." The CTO asks: "What's our brand voice?" Jordan says: "We're working on that." He has not started working on that.

**9:00 AM** -- The CEO drops by his desk (open floor plan). "Hey, I got us a speaking slot at a construction tech conference in 6 weeks. Can you make a deck and some leave-behinds? Also, the sales team needs new one-pagers. And can you update the website? The About page still has the old team photo with 3 people who left." Jordan writes down 4 new tasks. His to-do list is now at 18 items.

**9:30 AM** -- Jordan tries to make a LinkedIn post for the company page. The last post was 6 weeks ago. It got 4 likes (two of them were employees). He stares at the blank post. What's the company voice? Professional? Casual? Technical? The CEO writes in a completely different tone than the previous intern. There's no documentation of what the brand should sound like. He writes something generic about "construction technology innovation" and posts it. He knows it's mediocre.

**10:00 AM** -- Opens Canva. Starts designing the one-pager the sales team needs. Realizes he doesn't have the official brand colors saved anywhere. Opens the website, uses a color picker extension to grab the blues. They don't match the blues in the existing one-pager. He picks the ones that look "close enough." The CEO will later say "that blue looks different" and Jordan will have no way to prove which blue is correct because there are no brand guidelines.

**11:00 AM** -- The head of sales sends a message: "A prospect asked for a case study. Do we have one?" They do not have a case study. They have 4 happy customers but no documentation of their success. Jordan adds "create case study template" to the list. Item 19.

**12:00 PM** -- Lunch at his desk. Opens ChatGPT. Types: "Create a brand voice guide for a B2B construction tech SaaS company." Gets something generic. Tries Claude. Gets something slightly better but still disconnected from what Gridline actually is. Tries feeding in the company's website copy. The AI uses it but the output feels like a remix of existing mediocrity, not a step up. He saves the document and titles it "Brand Voice Draft -- DO NOT SHARE YET."

**1:00 PM** -- 1:1 with the CEO. The CEO shows Jordan a competitor's website. "They just relaunched. Look how clean this is. We need to look like this." Jordan nods. He doesn't say: "We have no brand system, no design resources, no photography, no documented voice, no competitive positioning, and I've been here 4 months trying to build all of this from scratch while also running social, email, sales collateral, the website, event prep, and customer marketing." He just nods.

**2:00 PM** -- Tries to brief a freelance designer on the conference deck. Realizes he can't brief them because he doesn't know what the brand should look like. He sends the designer the website link, 3 different logo files, and the note: "Just make it look clean and professional." He knows this will result in 2 rounds of revisions where the CEO says "this doesn't feel like us" without being able to articulate why.

**3:30 PM** -- Gets a cold email from a branding agency. "$25K brand identity package. Includes logo, color palette, typography, brand guidelines, and a one-page messaging framework. 8-week timeline." He forwards it to the CEO with the note "FYI -- thoughts?" The CEO responds: "That seems like a lot. Can't we just do this ourselves?" Jordan closes his laptop and stares at the wall for 3 minutes.

**4:00 PM** -- Back to the one-pager. He finishes a draft. It looks okay but not great. He sends it to the sales team. The head of sales says "Love it, but can you make the green darker? And add a QR code?" The green on the one-pager doesn't match the green on the website because there are apparently two greens and nobody decided which one is right.

**5:30 PM** -- Logs off. Texts his girlfriend: "Late again. Sorry." Goes to the gym instead of basketball. Runs on the treadmill and thinks about whether this job was a mistake.

**8:00 PM** -- Opens his laptop "for just 30 minutes." Googles: "how to build a brand system for a startup." Finds a blog post about brand operating systems. Reads it twice. Thinks: "This is what I've been trying to build but I can't build it AND execute at the same time." Bookmarks it.

**10:30 PM** -- Bed. Sets alarm. Falls asleep thinking: "I have 90 days to prove this hire was worth it and I'm spending 80% of my time on production tasks because there's no system."

## Decision Triggers

1. **The overwhelm wall.** Jordan realizes he literally cannot build the brand system AND execute marketing at the same time. Something has to give. He starts looking for help.
2. **The CEO comparison moment.** The CEO shows him a competitor's rebrand and says "why don't we look like this?" Jordan needs to either explain the infrastructure gap or fix it.
3. **The asset chaos emergency.** A customer or partner requests brand guidelines and Jordan doesn't have them. The embarrassment forces action.
4. **Seeing the system.** Jordan encounters Brand HQ and has the recognition moment: "This is the system I've been trying to build manually." He doesn't need convincing -- he needs to see it exists.
5. **Peer recommendation.** Another first-marketing-hire in a Slack community or LinkedIn post mentions the solution that saved them. Jordan trusts peers above agencies.
