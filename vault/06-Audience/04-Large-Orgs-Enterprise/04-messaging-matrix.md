## Per-Channel Tactics

### Warm Referral Network (Primary — 80% of Pipeline)

**Strategy:** Every enterprise deal starts with a warm introduction. Build the referral engine systematically.

**Tactical Steps:**
1. Map all existing connections to 500+ employee companies. Reagan's Idea Ranch network, Under Armour connections, Lucy's San Francisco network, existing clients.
2. For each connection, identify: Who is the marketing leader? What is their known pain point? Who is the warm connector?
3. Equip connectors with a single sentence they can forward (not a paragraph, not a deck — one sentence).
4. After introduction, follow up within 24 hours with a personalized note referencing the connector and one specific observation about the target company's brand.

**Warm Introduction Script:**

> "[Name], I want to connect you with [Reagan/Lucy]. They've built something I think you'll find relevant — a creative operating system that a couple of companies with the complexity you're managing are using. It gives marketing directors a brand intelligence system that actually accumulates context over time instead of resetting every time a vendor changes. Worth a quick call."

### LinkedIn (Credibility & Nurture)

**Targeting:** VP Marketing, CMO, Director of Brand, Head of Marketing at companies with 500+ employees. Manual engagement, not ads.

**Content Strategy:**
- Personal account posts from Lucy and Reagan (never from the company page for this tier — enterprise buyers follow people, not brands)
- Topics that signal enterprise experience:
  - "Why brand unification projects fail (and what actually works)"
  - "The hidden cost of agency rotation: what happens to your brand context"
  - "What your board sees when they look at your brand versus what you see"
  - "Why the marketing director is always the last person to get resources and the first person to get blamed"
- Format: Long-form text posts (600-1,200 words). No images needed. No carousels. Substance over format.
- Engagement: Comment thoughtfully on target buyers' posts. Build familiarity over 4-8 weeks before any outreach.

### Private Executive Dinners (Relationship Building)

**Format:** 8-12 marketing leaders. Hosted by StartSuite. Held during or adjacent to a relevant conference.

**Topic:** Not a pitch. A genuine conversation about "the challenges of brand management at scale." Let the marketing leaders share war stories. StartSuite facilitates, demonstrates empathy and expertise, and follows up individually.

**Outcome:** 2-3 attendees become warm leads. 4-5 become long-term network nodes. The investment ($3K-$5K for dinner) yields pipeline worth $100K+ over 12 months.

### Trade Publication Authored Content

**Target Publications:** Industry-specific trade journals relevant to target verticals (construction trades, consumer goods, media, etc.)

**Content:** Authored articles by Lucy or Reagan. Not about StartSuite — about brand operations challenges in their industry. Position the principals as peers to the buyers, not vendors selling to them.

**Example Topic:** "How a 100-Year Company Rebuilt Its Brand for the Next Century" (once Dunnick work is publishable)

## Token/Credit Model Messaging (Enterprise Version)

### The Problem to Name

> "You have spent $500K+ on agency retainers over the past five years. How much of that value still exists inside your company today? The agencies left. The account managers rotated. The PDFs are in a folder somewhere. The institutional knowledge walked out the door."

### The Reframe

> "StartSuite inverts the model. Every dollar you invest builds permanent infrastructure inside YOUR company. Brand HQ is yours. The competitive intelligence is yours. The context vault is yours. Credits are spent on specific outputs — and unused credits roll forward. You will never again pay for time someone did not spend on your brand."

### Enterprise-Specific Credit Messaging

1. **"Enterprise-scale flexibility."** Allocate credits across divisions. One division needs heavy support this quarter while another is in maintenance mode. Credits flow to where the need is, not where a retainer was pre-allocated.
2. **"Transparent reporting."** Monthly credit utilization report: what was spent, what was produced, what remains. Exportable. Auditable. No mysteries.
3. **"Capacity planning."** Annual credit packages with quarterly true-ups. If your needs grow, add capacity. If they shrink, roll forward. The system matches your reality.
4. **"Multi-division governance."** Each division can have its own credit allocation and usage dashboard within the master Brand HQ. Division leaders see their spend. Morgan sees everything.

## Objection Handling

### "We already have an agency. Several, actually."

> "You probably should keep them — for now. We are not here to replace your agencies. We are here to build the intelligence layer underneath them. Your agencies produce assets. Brand HQ ensures those assets are consistent across all your divisions, grounded in competitive intelligence, and documented with strategic rationale. Most of our enterprise clients keep their agencies and find the agencies actually produce better work because they finally have a proper brief."

### "How do I know this will work at our scale?"

> "Fair question. We are currently working with a 700+ employee, 100-year company managing multiple sub-brands across heavy civil construction, golf, water management, and real estate. The marketing director has a team of 3 and is responsible for the brand across all divisions. If we can handle that complexity, we can handle yours. I would be happy to walk you through the specifics."

### "Our procurement process is long and complicated."

> "We expected that. Here is our pre-completed vendor questionnaire. Here is our security posture document. Here is our MSA template, built to anticipate enterprise redlines. And here is my direct number for your procurement team. We will not let paperwork slow down your timeline."

### "I need this to work within our existing tech stack."

> "Brand HQ is a platform, not an integration. It does not replace your DAM, your CMS, or your project management tools. It sits alongside them as the intelligence and governance layer. Think of it as the brain that makes all your existing tools smarter — not another tool competing for space."

### "What happens if we end the engagement?"

> "Everything stays with you. Brand HQ is client-owned. The competitive intelligence, the context vault, the decision logs, the brand architecture — all of it. We do not hold intellectual property hostage. If we end the engagement, you keep the system and the data. Our job is to make the system so valuable that you never want to leave — not to trap you in a contract."

### "I have been through this with other vendors. What makes you different?"

> "I understand the skepticism, and I would not try to convince you with words. I would rather build a section of Brand HQ with your actual brand data and let you see the difference. If it does not feel fundamentally different from what you have experienced before, I will tell you. We are not for every company. But for companies at your scale with your complexity and your team size, we have built something that structurally cannot exist in a traditional agency model. The operating system belongs to you. The context accumulates permanently. That is architecturally different, not just rhetorically different."

### "Our CEO/board will ask about ROI."

> "Here is how to frame it. Option A: Hire 3 additional marketing staff ($450K/year fully loaded). Option B: Expand current agency retainer to cover the scope gap ($150K-$200K/year, with 30-40% waste). Option C: StartSuite Foundations + Add-ons ($60K-$120K/year), which delivers competitive intelligence, brand governance, AI workflows, AND builds permanent infrastructure your company owns. The ROI is not just cost savings — it is the compounding value of institutional knowledge that no longer walks out the door when a person or agency leaves."
