## Segment Definition

| Field | Detail |
|-------|--------|
| Company Size | 500+ employees |
| Revenue | $200M+ |
| Stage | Established, often multi-generational. Profitable. Multiple divisions, subsidiaries, or business units. Brand team exists but is dramatically under-resourced relative to company scale. |
| Org Structure | Marketing director or VP Marketing with a team of 3-8, serving a company that would require a team of 15-25 to manage properly. Often one person is the single thread connecting brand strategy to executive leadership. |
| Brand State | The brand is large, visible, and has equity. But the infrastructure behind it is held together by one person's institutional memory, scattered files, inconsistent sub-brands, and a patchwork of agency relationships. |

---

## Why They Matter

This is StartSuite's highest-revenue-per-account tier. These companies:

1. **Have real budgets.** $10K/month is a rounding error on their marketing spend. Price sensitivity is low. Sophistication sensitivity is high. They will pay for capability they believe in.
2. **Have massive complexity.** Multiple divisions, sub-brands, geographic markets, compliance requirements, and stakeholder groups. The brand challenges here are structural, not aesthetic.
3. **Create lock-in.** Once Brand HQ is embedded in a 500+ person company's workflows, switching costs are enormous. The context accumulation moat is deepest here.
4. **Validate the platform.** Having a 700+ employee, 100-year-old company on the client roster immediately legitimizes StartSuite for every tier below it.

## Sales Cycle

| Phase | Duration | Key Activity |
|-------|----------|-------------|
| Introduction | Week 1-2 | Warm introduction via mutual connection. NOT cold outreach. These buyers do not respond to cold emails. |
| Discovery | Week 2-4 | Deep discovery call. Understand org structure, brand challenges, existing vendor landscape, decision-making process. |
| Proof of Concept | Week 4-6 | Build a sample Brand HQ section using publicly available information about their brand. Show them what the system looks like with THEIR data, not a generic demo. |
| Stakeholder Alignment | Week 6-8 | Meet with 2-4 additional stakeholders. Tailor the pitch to each. Provide internal champion (marketing director) with materials to build the case. |
| Procurement | Week 8-10 | Vendor questionnaire, security review, legal negotiation, MSA. This is not optional — it will happen and must be anticipated. |
| Decision | Week 10-12 | Final approval. Budget allocation. SOW execution. Onboarding schedule. |

**Total: 6-12 weeks from introduction to signed contract.**

## Segment Signals (How to Identify Them)

- LinkedIn profile shows "Director of Marketing" or "VP Marketing" at a company with 500+ employees
- Company has multiple web properties, sub-brands, or divisions
- Marketing team job postings suggest under-staffing relative to company size
- Recent executive hire in marketing (signal: leadership knows brand needs investment)
- Industry presence at conferences but weak digital brand relative to company scale
- Agency relationships visible on LinkedIn (current agency team members connected to the company) — signal: they are already spending on brand, just not efficiently
- Public statements about "rebrand," "brand unification," or "digital transformation"

## Key Insight

> "Once Brand HQ is built for a client, StartSuite knows more about that brand than any other agency could on arrival." — Board Meeting Notes

For Tier 4, this insight is existential. These companies have spent years building brand knowledge that lives in one person's head. When that person leaves, the knowledge leaves. StartSuite becomes the permanent institutional memory — the thing that survives personnel changes, agency rotations, and leadership transitions. That is worth $10K/month to a company spending $200M+ in revenue.
