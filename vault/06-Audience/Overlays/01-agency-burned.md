# Psychographic Overlay: Agency-Burned

**Overlay:** 01 -- Agency-Burned
**Last Updated:** March 26, 2026
**Source:** Buyer Signal Mining Report, Nicole/Dunnick transcripts, Board Meeting Transcript

---

## What This Overlay Means

"Agency-burned" buyers have been through one or more agency relationships that left them feeling overcharged, underdelivered, or taken advantage of. This isn't skepticism about outsourcing -- it's scar tissue. They've spent real money, waited real time, and received outputs that didn't match the pitch. The result is a buyer who is simultaneously:
- More sophisticated (they've learned what to look for)
- More guarded (they expect to be disappointed)
- More demanding (they need proof, not promises)
- More receptive to the right offer (they know exactly what they want, and it's the opposite of what they had)

## Signal Detection

### Phrases They Use

| What They Say | What They Mean |
|---------------|----------------|
| "I don't like retainers" | "I've paid for months where I got nothing." |
| "I just want transparency" | "My last agency was a black box." |
| "I've worked with agencies before" | Warning: this is not neutral. It's a flag that they have baggage. |
| "Use it or lose it is unfair" | They've been on retainer models where unused hours expired. |
| "I don't want to feel taken advantage of" | They already have. Probably more than once. |
| "I want to own everything" | They've lost source files, design assets, or IP when leaving an agency. |
| "Can you explain what I'm paying for?" | They've been billed for vague deliverables before. |
| "I don't need more pretty things. I need a system." | They've received beautiful creative with zero strategic foundation. |
| "What happens when I cancel?" | They've been trapped in contracts or hit with exit fees. |

### Behaviors That Signal This Overlay

- Ask detailed pricing questions early (they've been surprised by hidden costs before)
- Request references or case studies before a second meeting
- Push back on scope creep or vague deliverables
- Want to see the work process, not just the output
- Compare StartSuite to their previous agency experience (positively or negatively)
- Test for ownership: "Do I get the source files?"
- Bring up specific bad experiences unprompted

## Tactical Notes for Sales Conversations

1. **Let them vent.** Agency-burned buyers often need to tell their story. Don't interrupt. Don't rush past it. Their frustration IS the buying signal.
2. **Mirror their language.** If they say "transparency," use "transparency." If they say "ownership," use "ownership." Don't introduce new jargon.
3. **Show the system immediately.** Don't start with a pitch deck. Open Brand HQ for an existing client (anonymized if needed). Let them see the living system. This is the fastest way to differentiate from their previous agency experience.
4. **Address the retainer question proactively.** Don't wait for them to bring it up. Say early: "We don't do retainers. Here's how our model works." The relief is visible.
5. **Offer the "keep everything" guarantee.** State explicitly: "If you ever leave, you take everything -- the Brand HQ, the strategy docs, the assets, the competitive intelligence. We don't hold anything." This removes the last barrier.
