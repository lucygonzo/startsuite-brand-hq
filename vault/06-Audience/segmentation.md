# Audience Segmentation

**Last Updated:** March 26, 2026
**Source:** brandData.ts audience section, project_target_clients.md

---

## Key Takeaway

StartSuite segments its audience by maturity and problem type. Four primary segments: early-stage startups who need a brand from scratch, growing companies with brand debt, mid-stage companies with new marketing leadership, and enterprise brand teams needing an AI-empowered creative operating system. The ICP is a founder or brand leader with context scattered across 10 tools who needs a system, not another agency.

---

## Primary Segments

### 1. Early-Stage Startups (Pre-Seed to Seed)

**Priority:** Highest

**Description:** Founders who need a brand built from scratch. Just closed their first round and need to look like they've raised more than they have. The most natural entry point for Brand Discovery. Real example: 247 Sports (pronounced 2047).

**Demographics:** 25-45 years old, technical or domain-expert founders, US-based

**Psychographics:** Ambitious, resource-constrained, skeptical of agencies, comfortable with technology, moves fast

**Pain Points:**
- I don't have a brand and need one fast
- Can't afford a full-time creative hire or a $50K agency engagement
- Past freelancer experiences were fragmented and inconsistent
- Brand looks like it was built in Canva by a non-designer

**Needs:**
- A brand that looks like they've raised more than they have
- A creative operating system that moves at startup speed
- A partner who understands the startup and investor context
- Brand intelligence they can use without a designer on staff

---

### 2. Growing Companies with Brand Debt (Series A+)

**Priority:** High

**Description:** Series A+ or established companies whose brand hasn't kept up with growth. Inconsistent assets, no brand guidelines, marketing spend with no strategic foundation. Real example: PhyCap (multiple sub-brands: Robin, Dr. Slowsar).

**Demographics:** 30-50 years old, experienced founders or professional CEOs, 10-50 employees

**Psychographics:** Growth-focused, brand-aware, willing to invest in quality, time-constrained

**Pain Points:**
- Brand doesn't match the company's current stage or ambition
- Inconsistent assets across a growing team with no brand guidelines
- Pitch materials don't match the quality of the product
- Marketing spend has no strategic foundation

**Needs:**
- A brand operating system that scales with the team
- Consistent assets and guidelines across all touchpoints
- Competitive intelligence to inform positioning decisions
- A strategic partner, not just a vendor

---

### 3. Mid-Stage with New Leadership

**Priority:** High

**Description:** Companies bringing in new marketing or brand leadership who need strategic grounding fast. The Nicole/Dunnick archetype: sophisticated, data-driven, anti-retainer, wants flexibility and control. Real example: Dunnick/Nicole (token model preference).

**Demographics:** 30-45 years old, marketing leaders (CMOs, Directors), hired into existing companies

**Psychographics:** Data-driven, politically aware, needs quick wins, skeptical of traditional retainers, sophisticated buyer

**Pain Points:**
- Inherited a brand with no strategy documentation or competitive analysis
- Need to justify brand investment to leadership with evidence
- Previous agency relationships were expensive and hard to measure
- Need to prove myself with data, not just creative

**Needs:**
- Brand intelligence and competitive data to build a business case
- A Brand HQ that documents strategy decisions and rationale
- Flexible engagement model (token/credit model for anti-retainer buyers)
- A partner who makes them look strategic, not just creative

---

### 4. Enterprise Brand Teams

**Priority:** Medium

**Description:** Large companies where internal brand teams need an AI-empowered creative operating system to augment their capabilities. Need strategic grounding, competitive intelligence, and a system that makes their existing team more effective. Real example: Costa Sunglasses (Jed, ex-Under Armour CMO).

**Demographics:** 35-55 years old, senior marketing and brand leaders, established companies

**Psychographics:** Strategic, process-oriented, budget-aware, value partnership and expertise, need to justify ROI

**Pain Points:**
- Internal team is stretched thin across too many initiatives
- Competitive intelligence is ad-hoc and reactive
- Brand decisions lack data-backed rationale
- Need external strategic perspective without losing internal control

**Needs:**
- An AI-empowered creative operating system that augments the internal team
- Competitive intelligence platform with ongoing monitoring
- Strategic partnership that elevates the existing brand team
- Brand HQ as a single source of truth that the whole team can access

## Ideal Client Profile (ICP)

A founder or brand leader who has context scattered across 10 tools, knows brand matters but can't articulate why, and needs a system that organizes their creative intelligence — not just another agency that creates more stuff. They are at an inflection point (just raised, just hired, just realized their brand is broken) and want a creative operating system, not a project-based vendor.

### ICP Signals

- Recently closed a funding round or hit a growth milestone
- Hiring marketing or brand leadership for the first time
- Preparing for a demo day, investor meeting, or product launch
- Brand is inconsistent across LinkedIn, pitch deck, and website
- Previous agency or freelancer experience was frustrating
- Referred by a VC, accelerator, or existing StartSuite client
- Asks for data and competitive context, not just pretty assets
- Context scattered across multiple tools with no single source of truth

## Segmentation by Problem Type (STP Framework)

| Problem Type | Description |
|---|---|
| No brand | "I have too many channels and ideas and no clarity on what's actually working" |
| Broken brand | "We have all this content but it doesn't feel cohesive and I can't explain why" |
| Undefended brand | "I'm being told to build a social presence but I don't know what good looks like and I can't defend my choices internally" |
| Drifted brand | "We've grown and our brand has drifted. Nobody knows what the rules are anymore" |

---

## Ideal Client Signals

- Excited and already wanting help, hungry to figure things out
- Self-starters and early adopters
- Have use cases they can articulate
- Need to outsource or want to outsource depending on size/expertise
- Managing multiple teams, juggling multiple things
