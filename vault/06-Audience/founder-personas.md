## Founder Segments

### Early-Stage Startups (Pre-Seed to Seed)

**Track:** Founder
**Priority:** Highest
**Segment Score:** Revenue 4, Strategic 5, Product Fit 5, Acquisition 4, Total 18

**Description:** Just closed their first round. Need a brand built from scratch. Problem type: "I don't have a brand and need one fast." The most natural Brand Discovery entry point. Real example: 247 Sports (pronounced 2047).

**Demographics:** 25-45, technical or domain-expert founders, US-based

**Psychographics:** Ambitious, resource-constrained, skeptical of agencies, comfortable with technology, moves fast

**Pain Points:**
- No brand, no positioning, no visual identity
- Can't afford a full-time creative hire
- Past freelancer/agency experiences were slow and fragmented
- Brand was thrown together in Canva or by a developer friend
- Inconsistent assets across LinkedIn, deck, and website

**Needs:**
- A brand that looks like they've raised more than they have
- A creative operating system that moves at startup speed
- A partner who understands the startup and investor context
- Brand intelligence they can use without a designer on staff

**Acquisition Channels:** 46Capital referrals, Accelerator partnerships, Founder-to-founder referrals, LinkedIn content

**Representative Quote:** "I need a brand that looks like we've raised a Series A, but we just closed our seed round last month."

## Detailed Persona: Marcus

**Name:** Marcus
**Title:** The Seed-Stage Founder
**Segment:** Early-Stage Startups

### Demographics

34, CEO of a B2B SaaS startup, just closed a $1.2M seed round, based in Tulsa

### Quote

"I need to look like we've raised a Series A before we actually raise one."

### Story

Marcus built his product over 18 months with a co-founder. They closed their seed round last quarter and now have 6 months of runway to prove the model. His LinkedIn profile still has the old logo. His pitch deck was designed by his co-founder in Keynote. His website was built by a developer friend.

### Goals

- Close Series A in 12 months
- Hire 3 key team members in the next 6 months
- Land 5 enterprise pilot customers
- Build a brand that attracts top talent and investors

### Challenges

- No time to manage a creative process
- Budget is tight — every dollar needs to move the needle
- Past freelancer experiences were slow and inconsistent
- Doesn't know what "good brand" looks like for his stage

### Motivations

- Wants investors to take him seriously at the next stage
- Wants to attract the right talent with a credible brand
- Wants a brand that reflects the quality of his product
- Wants to stop being embarrassed by his website and deck

### Day in the Life

Marcus wakes up at 6am and checks Slack before getting out of bed. He has 3 investor meetings this week and a demo day next month. He spent 2 hours last weekend trying to update his pitch deck and gave up. He knows his brand is holding him back but doesn't know where to start.

### How StartSuite Helps

StartSuite starts with Brand Discovery — a deep research process that produces Marcus's living Brand HQ with competitive intelligence, audience data, and a strategic foundation. Then Foundations at $3K/month keeps the system running and compounding as he grows.

## Detailed Persona: Nicole

**Name:** Nicole
**Title:** The New Marketing Director
**Segment:** Mid-Stage with New Leadership

### Demographics

38, VP of Marketing, just hired at a Series B company with 35 employees, inheriting a messy brand

### Quote

"I need data, not just design. I need to prove that brand investment moves the business."

### Story

Nicole was hired 3 months ago to professionalize the marketing function. The company has grown fast but the brand is inconsistent — different teams use different logos, messaging varies across sales decks, and there's no competitive positioning strategy. Her CEO wants results in 90 days.

### Goals

- Audit and restructure the brand within 90 days
- Build a data-backed case for brand investment to the CEO
- Create brand guidelines that the entire team will actually follow
- Establish competitive positioning that differentiates in the market

### Challenges

- No brand documentation exists — everything is in people's heads
- Previous agency relationships were project-based and expensive
- Needs to show ROI quickly to maintain budget and credibility
- Anti-retainer — wants flexibility and control, not a long-term lock-in

### Motivations

- Wants to be seen as the leader who professionalized the brand
- Wants competitive intelligence to make better strategic decisions
- Wants a system that her team can use independently
- Wants a flexible engagement model, not a traditional retainer

### How StartSuite Helps

Brand Discovery gives Nicole the competitive intelligence and brand audit she needs to build her case to leadership. Brand HQ becomes the living documentation system. The token/credit model for add-ons gives her the flexibility she demands.
