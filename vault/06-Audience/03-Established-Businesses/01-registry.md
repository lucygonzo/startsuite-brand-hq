# Tier 3: Established Businesses — Segment Registry

**Tier Name:** Established Businesses
**Priority:** PRIMARY
**Status:** CONFIRMED (247 Sports, Costa Sunglasses)
**Last Updated:** March 26, 2026

---

## Segment Definition

| Field | Detail |
|-------|--------|
| Company Size | 50-500 employees |
| Revenue | $20M-$200M |
| Stage | Profitable or well-funded. Multiple teams, multiple channels, often multiple sub-brands. Brand has not kept pace with operational growth. |
| Org Structure | Dedicated marketing person or small team (2-5), but under-resourced relative to company complexity. Often one director managing social, content, web, events, internal comms, and vendor relationships simultaneously. |
| Brand State | They HAVE a brand. It exists. But it is fragmented, inconsistent, managed reactively, and owned by one overwhelmed person who inherited it from whoever had the job before them. |

---

## Why They Matter

This is StartSuite's highest-volume tier for Foundations revenue. These companies:

1. **Already have budget.** They are not scraping together funds. Marketing line items exist. The question is allocation, not ability to pay.
2. **Already feel the pain.** They know something is wrong. Three different greens on the website. Five taglines across four channels. A Google Drive with 800 logo variants. They live with inconsistency every day.
3. **Don't need brand from scratch.** They need a SYSTEM. Brand Discovery is not the entry point here — Foundations is. They have a brand. They need it organized, governed, and operationalized.
4. **Have immediate use cases.** Unlike early-stage companies that need education on why brand matters, established businesses can articulate specific problems: "Our sub-brands are inconsistent," "We can't onboard a new designer without losing a month," "I can't defend my marketing decisions to the CEO."

## Segment Signals (How to Identify Them)

- LinkedIn title: Marketing Director, Brand Manager, VP Marketing, Head of Marketing
- Company has 50-500 employees on LinkedIn
- Multiple web properties or sub-brands visible
- Active on LinkedIn but content is inconsistent in quality and tone
- Job postings for marketing roles (signal: under-resourced team)
- Recently hired a marketing director (signal: they know they have a problem)
- Mention of "rebrand" or "brand refresh" in company news
- Working with 1-2 agencies already but dissatisfied

---

## Key Insight

> "Nobody ever comes and says, 'I don't have a brand voice.' They say, 'I need posts.'" — Lucy

The established business buyer does not self-identify as having a brand strategy problem. They self-identify as having a content problem, a consistency problem, or a "my team is overwhelmed" problem. The sale happens when StartSuite surfaces the root cause through questions, not assertions, and the buyer has the "spark in the eye" moment of realizing what they actually need.
