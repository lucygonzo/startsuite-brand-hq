## Funnel Leaks (Where We Lose Them)

### Leak 1: "Is this for startups only?"

**Where it happens:** First impression — website, LinkedIn, initial conversation.
**Why it happens:** StartSuite's name, positioning, and early case studies skew toward startups. An established business marketing director sees "StartSuite" and assumes it is for companies that are just starting.
**How to fix it:** Case studies featuring companies at this tier. Testimonials from marketing directors, not founders. Language on the website that explicitly says "for growing companies and established businesses" — not just "for startups."
**Severity:** HIGH — this is the single biggest perception barrier for Tier 3.

### Leak 2: "Can you handle our complexity?"

**Where it happens:** Discovery call or proposal stage.
**Why it happens:** Established businesses have multiple brands, sub-brands, compliance requirements, existing brand guidelines, existing agency relationships, and internal politics. They need to see that StartSuite has dealt with complexity before.
**How to fix it:** The 247 Sports case study is the answer. 165+ programs, 3 different greens, no brand owner — and StartSuite built Brand HQ in 2 days. Lead with this. Also: the FICAP house-of-brands example (Robin, Dr. Slowsar) shows sub-brand management capability.
**Severity:** HIGH

### Leak 3: Procurement and Legal Process

**Where it happens:** After the marketing director says yes but before the contract is signed.
**Why it happens:** Companies at this size have procurement departments, legal review processes, and vendor onboarding procedures. A marketing director who loves StartSuite may not have authority to sign without 2-4 additional approvals.
**How to fix it:** Provide a procurement-ready one-pager: pricing, scope, data security posture, insurance, references. Make it easy for the marketing director to champion StartSuite internally. Build the internal case FOR them.
**Severity:** MEDIUM — not a deal-killer, but adds 2-4 weeks to the sales cycle.

### Leak 4: Multiple Stakeholders

**Where it happens:** Mid-sales cycle.
**Why it happens:** The marketing director is rarely the sole decision-maker. The CEO, CFO, or VP of Operations often has a say. Each stakeholder has different priorities: the marketing director wants capability, the CFO wants ROI, the CEO wants to know it will not create more problems.
**How to fix it:** Create stakeholder-specific messaging. For the CFO: "This replaces the equivalent of an 8-10 person agency team at a fraction of the cost." For the CEO: "Brand HQ is the single source of truth your marketing director has been asking for." For the VP Ops: "This reduces the number of marketing vendors you need to manage."
**Severity:** MEDIUM

### Leak 5: "Let me think about it" (Decision Paralysis)

**Where it happens:** After the demo, before the commitment.
**Why it happens:** These buyers have been burned before. They have hired agencies that over-promised. They have bought tools that went unused. They are sophisticated enough to be cautious. "Let me think about it" is often code for "I need to see more proof" or "I need to build the internal case."
**How to fix it:** Offer a paid pilot. A 30-day Foundations engagement at reduced scope. Let them experience Brand HQ with their actual brand data before committing to a full engagement. Reduce the risk of the decision.
**Severity:** MEDIUM

## Content Preferences

### What They Consume (and Share Internally)

1. **Case studies with similar-sized companies.** Not startup case studies. Not enterprise case studies. Companies their size, in their situation. "We helped a 200-person media company organize 165 sub-brands into one system" is more powerful than "We helped a startup get their first logo."

2. **Sophistication signals.** The quality of StartSuite's own Brand HQ is the proof. If the marketing director visits Brand HQ and sees something more polished than their own brand, they immediately understand the capability gap. Brand HQ IS the case study.

3. **ROI data.** Not vanity metrics. Hard numbers: "Reduced brand asset retrieval time from 45 minutes to 2 minutes." "Eliminated the need for a $150K creative director hire." "Cut agency spend by 40% while increasing content output by 3x."

4. **Competitive intelligence samples.** The competitive landscape research is the single strongest demo asset for this tier. Show them what their competitors are doing that they are not. The data is expensive elsewhere and free inside Foundations.

5. **Process documentation.** These buyers want to understand HOW the engagement works, not just WHAT they get. A clear onboarding timeline, deliverable schedule, and communication cadence reduces anxiety and builds trust.

### What They Ignore

- Generic "brand is important" content (they already know)
- AI hype without specifics (they are fatigued by AI marketing)
- Founder-focused messaging (they are employees, not founders)
- Short-form social content (too shallow for their decision process)

## Win Factors (Why They Choose StartSuite)

1. **Operating system framing, not project-based.** The biggest differentiator. Agencies sell projects. StartSuite sells a system. Established businesses are tired of project-based relationships that end and leave nothing behind. The operating system is permanent infrastructure.

2. **Can handle multiple brands.** The house-of-brands capability (demonstrated with FICAP/Robin/Dr. Slowsar) is directly relevant. Many companies at this tier manage 2-5 brands and struggle to keep them consistent.

3. **Competitive intelligence.** StartSuite knows more about their competitive landscape than they do. This is immediately valuable and hard to replicate.

4. **Speed of delivery.** Brand HQ built in 2 days for 247 Sports. Entire product reskinned in 2 weeks for the Nashville client. These timelines are 10-20x faster than traditional agencies.

5. **Context accumulation.** The longer a client stays, the deeper the intelligence. This creates genuine switching costs — no new agency can replicate 6+ months of accumulated brand context on arrival.

6. **The "defend myself" factor.** Marketing directors at established businesses live under scrutiny. Brand HQ gives them an audit trail, a decision log, and a rationale for every choice. They can walk into any meeting and justify their decisions with data.

---

## Loss Factors (Why They Choose Something Else)

1. **Perceived as a "startup agency."** If the first impression is "this is for companies getting started," an established business walks away immediately. The name "StartSuite" reinforces this perception.

2. **No enterprise case studies yet.** The confirmed case studies (247 Sports, Costa) are strong but not yet published. Until they are documented and presentable, the established business buyer has to take a leap of faith.

3. **No SOC2 or security posture.** Companies at this size increasingly require vendor security assessments. A SOC2 certification or at minimum a security questionnaire response is expected. Without it, procurement may block the engagement regardless of marketing's enthusiasm.

4. **Agency inertia.** Many established businesses already have an agency. Even if they are unhappy, switching costs are real: the agency holds institutional knowledge, has relationships with team members, and switching requires effort.

5. **Committee decision-making.** Unlike a founder who can decide in a single meeting, established business purchases require alignment across 2-5 stakeholders. Each stakeholder adds friction and extends the timeline.

## Competitive Alternatives (What They Use Instead)

### Option 1: Hire an In-House Creative Director ($150K-$200K/year)

- **Pros:** Dedicated, full-time, learns the brand deeply over time.
- **Cons:** Single point of failure. Takes 3-6 months to ramp. No competitive intelligence capability. No operating system — just another person managing the same chaos.
- **StartSuite counter:** "For less than the cost of one hire, you get a creative operating system, competitive intelligence, AI workflows, and a team that can ramp in days, not months."

### Option 2: Traditional Agency (Pentagram, local shops, regional agencies)

- **Pros:** Established process. Known deliverables. Portfolio of work to evaluate.
- **Cons:** Retainer model (use-it-or-lose-it). Context resets when account teams turn over. No operating system — they produce assets, not infrastructure. Expensive for what they deliver.
- **StartSuite counter:** "Agencies produce assets. We build the system that produces assets. You own the system. The context stays with you, not with us."

### Option 3: In-House Team + Canva/AI Tools

- **Pros:** Cheap. Fast for simple assets. Team has direct brand knowledge.
- **Cons:** No strategy layer. No competitive intelligence. No consistency governance. The "first 80% is easy, last 20% is impossible" problem. Every person using Canva makes slightly different choices.
- **StartSuite counter:** "Canva gives you templates. We give you the brand intelligence that tells you which template to use, what to say, and why. The context layer is what makes every output on-brand, not just on-template."

### Option 4: Do Nothing / Status Quo

- **Pros:** No cost. No change management. No new vendor to manage.
- **Cons:** The brand continues to fragment. The marketing director continues to be overwhelmed. Competitors who invest in brand pull further ahead. The cost of inaction compounds.
- **StartSuite counter:** Show them the gap. Pull up their website and count the inconsistencies. Show them what competitors are doing. Make the cost of inaction tangible and immediate.
