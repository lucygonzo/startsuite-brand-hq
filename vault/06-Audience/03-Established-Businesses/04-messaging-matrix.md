**Audience:** Marketing Directors at companies with 50-500 employees

**Last Updated:** March 26, 2026

## Per-Channel Tactics

### LinkedIn (Primary Acquisition Channel)

**Targeting:**
- Title: Marketing Director, VP Marketing, Brand Manager, Head of Brand, Director of Marketing Communications
- Company size: 50-500 employees
- Industry: All (but prioritize media, consumer brands, professional services, manufacturing, construction)
- Geography: US-focused initially

**Content Strategy:**
- 2-3 posts per week from StartSuite company page
- 1 long-form post per week from Lucy or Reagan's personal accounts (thought leadership outperforms company page 5:1)
- Content themes:
  - "Why your brand feels harder than it should" (problem awareness)
  - "What your competitors know about you" (competitive intelligence tease)
  - "The real cost of brand inconsistency" (ROI content)
  - "What a brand operating system actually looks like" (product education)
  - Case study snippets from 247 Sports (once publishable)

**Content Format:**
- Text posts with a single insight (not listicles)
- Carousels showing before/after brand system comparisons
- Short video (60-90 seconds) showing Brand HQ in action
- No memes. No "growth hacking" language. No emojis in professional posts.

**Engagement Rules:**
- Comment on posts from target marketing directors. Not sales comments — insight comments.
- Share and add perspective to relevant industry content
- DM only after 3+ engagement touchpoints (never cold DM)

### Direct Outreach via Warm Introduction (Highest Conversion Channel)

**Process:**
1. Identify target company and marketing director through LinkedIn research
2. Check network for mutual connections (Reagan's Idea Ranch network, Lucy's San Francisco network, existing client referrals)
3. Ask mutual connection for a warm introduction — provide them a single sentence they can forward
4. If no mutual connection exists, engage on LinkedIn for 2-4 weeks before any outreach

**Warm Introduction Script (for the connector to send):**

> "Hey [Name] — I want to connect you with [Reagan/Lucy] from StartSuite. They built something I've never seen before: a brand operating system that gives marketing directors a single source of truth for everything — brand guidelines, competitive intelligence, content strategy, the works. I think it would be relevant given what you're managing. Worth 15 minutes?"

### Conference Presence

**Target Conferences:**
- Industry-specific marketing conferences (not startup conferences)
- ANA (Association of National Advertisers) events
- Regional marketing summits
- Industry vertical conferences where marketing directors attend (construction, sports, consumer products)

**Approach:**
- Speaking slots over booth presence (thought leadership over logo visibility)
- Topic: "Why your brand needs an operating system, not another agency" — directly addresses the agency-burned buyer
- Post-session follow-up: personalized LinkedIn message referencing the session, offering a Brand HQ walkthrough

### Email Newsletter (Nurture Channel)

**Name:** Brand Intelligence Brief
**Frequency:** Monthly
**Content:**
- 1 competitive intelligence insight (anonymized industry trend)
- 1 brand operations tip (specific, tactical, implementable)
- 1 case study snippet or client success metric
- 0 sales pitches. The value IS the pitch.

## Token/Credit Model Messaging (For Anti-Retainer Buyers)

This is critical for Tier 3. Nicole's "I don't like retainers" objection is the norm, not the exception.

### The Problem to Name

> "You've paid for retainers where you used 60% of the value and lost the rest. You've been told 'use it or lose it.' You've watched agencies bill for hours they didn't earn. We get it. That model is broken."

### The Reframe

> "StartSuite uses a credit system. Credits roll over. You use them when you need them, for what you need them for. No mystery hours. No phantom deliverables. Every credit maps to a specific output. You see exactly where your investment goes."

### Specific Messaging Points

1. **"Credits, not hours."** We do not bill for time. We bill for outputs. A competitive analysis is X credits. A campaign brief is Y credits. You choose what to spend on.
2. **"Roll-over, not lose-it."** Unused credits carry forward. If you have a quiet month, your credits wait for you. No more paying for value you did not receive.
3. **"Transparent tracking."** Every credit spent is logged in Brand HQ. You can see your balance, your usage history, and what was produced at any time.
4. **"Scale up or down."** Need more capacity for a product launch? Add credits for that month. Quiet quarter? Use your baseline. The system flexes with your reality.

## Objection Handling

### "We already have an agency."

> "Good. We are not trying to replace them. An agency produces assets. We build the operating system that makes sure every asset — whether your agency makes it, your team makes it, or AI makes it — is consistent, on-brand, and defensible. Most of our clients at your size keep their agency and use StartSuite as the intelligence layer underneath."

### "Is this for startups?"

> "The name might suggest that, but no. Our strongest work is with established companies — CBS Sports properties, Luxottica brands, multi-division companies with 50-700+ employees. We work with companies that already have a brand but don't have a system to manage it at their current scale."

### "I need to get approval from [CEO/CFO/procurement]."

> "Expected. Here is a one-pager built for your CEO that focuses on competitive intelligence and ROI. Here is a version for your CFO that compares our cost to an in-house creative director hire. And here is our security and vendor questionnaire, pre-filled. We want to make it easy for you to champion this internally."

### "What about our existing brand guidelines?"

> "We start with them. Brand HQ does not replace your guidelines — it operationalizes them. Your existing brand book becomes the foundation. We organize it, fill the gaps, add competitive intelligence, build the voice system, and create a living document that updates as your brand evolves. Nothing is lost. Everything is elevated."

### "This seems expensive for what it is."

> "Let me reframe. A full-time creative director at a company your size costs $150K-$200K per year. A traditional agency retainer for this level of service runs $8K-$15K per month. StartSuite Foundations is $3K per month, and it includes competitive intelligence, brand governance, AI workflows, and a system that accumulates value over time. The question is not whether $3K is a lot. The question is what $3K replaces."

### "How is this different from Canva / Notion / a shared Google Drive?"

> "Those are tools. They store things. They do not think. StartSuite builds the intelligence layer that tells your team what to create, why, and how it connects to your broader strategy. A Google Drive does not know your competitive landscape. Canva does not know your brand voice. Notion does not accumulate context over time. We do."
