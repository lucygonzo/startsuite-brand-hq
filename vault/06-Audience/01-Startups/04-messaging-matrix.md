## Per-Channel Tactics

### LinkedIn

**What to post:** Founder-facing content that names the specific pains they feel. Not "brand is important" (generic). Instead: "Your pitch deck has 3 different fonts and your investor noticed."

**Tone:** Direct, specific, slightly provocative. Not salesy. Teach, don't pitch. Show taste through the content itself.

**Frequency:** 3--4x/week from Lucy and Reagan's personal accounts. Company page reposts.

**Example hooks:**
- "Your investor said 'tighten up the brand.' Here's what they actually mean."
- "I rebranded a 300,000-user product in 2 weeks. Here's the exact process."
- "Your startup doesn't have a branding problem. It has a brand infrastructure problem. Here's the difference."
- "3 things wrong with every seed-stage pitch deck (and the 10-minute fix for each)."

**Format:** Text posts with a single image (before/after, brand audit screenshot, or Brand HQ demo). Carousels for tactical content. No stock photos. No generic graphics.

### Email

**Subject line style:** Specific and curiosity-driven. Not "Brand tips for startups" -- instead "The brand mistake your investor won't tell you about" or "What 3 different greens on your website actually costs you."

**Content angle:** One problem, one insight, one CTA. Short (150--250 words). Written like a founder talking to a founder, not a marketer talking to a prospect.

**CTA:** "See what Brand HQ looks like for a startup like yours" (link to demo or case study). Never "Schedule a consultation" -- that sounds like an agency.

**Cadence:** 1x/week for nurture. Triggered emails after demo page visit or case study download.

### Accelerator Intros

**How to pitch to a program manager:** Lead with the value to their portfolio, not to StartSuite.

**Script:** "Your companies raise money, file an LLC, get a bank account, and set up QuickBooks. The one thing missing from that stack is brand infrastructure. We built a system that gives every portfolio company a living brand guide, competitive intelligence, and a messaging framework in their first 30 days. We'll offer your cohort a subsidized rate and we'll track which companies actually use it -- that data feeds back to you."

**Key framing:** StartSuite as portfolio infrastructure, not a vendor recommendation. Tracy offered to have 46 Capital pay first-year subscriptions. That model scales to any fund.

**Ask:** A 15-minute slot at a cohort kickoff or demo day. One Brand HQ demo for a portfolio company as proof of concept.

### Website

**What this person needs to see on the homepage:**

1. **Above the fold:** A headline that names their pain ("Your brand is making first impressions without you") + a 10-second Brand HQ demo GIF or screenshot + "See pricing" and "Book a call" buttons
2. **Social proof bar:** Logos of startup clients + "Backed by 46 Capital"
3. **How it works:** 3 steps, each with a time estimate. "Day 1: Strategy call. Day 5: Brand HQ draft. Day 10: Live brand system."
4. **Before/after:** One startup transformation with specific details (raised $X after rebrand, reduced content creation time by Y%)
5. **Pricing:** Starting at $3K/mo for Foundations. Brand Discovery from $2.5K one-time. No "contact us for pricing."
6. **Testimonial:** One founder quote, specific, with their name and company

**What they should NOT see:** Jargon ("creative operating system" without context), long paragraphs, stock photography, agency-speak ("we craft bespoke brand experiences"), or anything that looks like it takes more than 60 seconds to understand.

### Objection 1: "We can't afford $3K/month right now."

Logo, brand guide, messaging framework, competitive landscape. It's [$X] and takes about 10 days. When you're ready for ongoing brand infrastructure, Foundations is there. ### Objection 2: "We'll just use Canva / do it ourselves."

**Response:** "You absolutely can. Most founders do for the first 6--12 months. The problem isn't the tool -- it's the strategy behind it. Canva can make a great social post if you know your voice, your colors, your messaging framework, and what your competitors are doing. Without that context, you get assets that look fine individually but feel inconsistent as a system. We build the context layer. Your team can use Canva, Figma, whatever they want -- but now they're working from a strategy, not vibes."

**Response:** "Great -- keep them. We're not replacing your designer. We're giving them the strategy and system to work from.
