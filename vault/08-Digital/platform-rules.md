## LinkedIn Rules of Engagement

**Role:** Primary channel. This is where the audience lives and makes business decisions.

**Cadence:** 3x/week per person (Reagan + Lucy personal brands as primary distribution)

**Content Rules:**
- Teaser copy that drives the "see more" click — front-load the insight
- Expert-level thought leadership only — every post must pass the expert insight test
- Declarative, confident tone — no hedging, no throat-clearing
- Use data and competitive context to support claims
- Bar for standing out is lower than assumed — quality > volume
- Personal brands (Reagan, Lucy) are the primary distribution, not the company page

**Format Guidelines:**
- Short-form text posts with a strong opening line ("grabber" not "hook")
- Carousel posts for frameworks and competitive breakdowns
- Quote graphics from shoot day material
- Occasional boosted posts when organic performance is strong (Phase 2 paid)

**What NOT to Post:**
- Generic motivational content
- Repurposed blog posts without platform-native formatting
- Content that reveals proprietary client details (discretion principle)
- Anything that doesn't pass the expert insight test

---

## YouTube Rules of Engagement

**Role:** Long-form authority channel. Builds depth that LinkedIn posts cannot achieve alone.

**Cadence:** 2-4x/month from monthly shoot day material

**Content Rules:**
- Watch time is the metric, NOT subscribers
- A 1-subscriber channel with 10M views outperforms a 1M-subscriber channel with low watch time
- Long-form educational content: 15-45 minute episodes
- War room internal discussions (authentic, low-fi)
- Expert guest interviews (structured, 2-hour raw recording edited down)

**Format Guidelines:**
- Monthly shoot day produces the raw material
- Hub-and-spoke: long-form episode becomes 3-5 short clips for other platforms
- Thumbnails and titles optimized for click-through
- Platform-specific editing (YouTube pacing is different from LinkedIn)

**What NOT to Post:**
- Short-form content native to YouTube Shorts (this is not the strategy)
- Content without substance — every video must teach something

---

## Instagram Rules of Engagement

**Role:** Presence only. Searchable landing page that redirects to LinkedIn/YouTube.

**Cadence:** 1-2x/week (repurposed content only)

**Content Rules:**
- No active content strategy — this is not a primary channel
- Brand work showcase and repurposed LinkedIn content
- Collab feature for client work attribution (exception to minimal-effort rule)
- Serves as a searchable landing page for prospects who check Instagram

**What NOT to Post:**
- Original content created specifically for Instagram
- Stories or Reels as a primary format (repurposed only)
- Content that competes for attention with LinkedIn or YouTube efforts

---

## TikTok Rules of Engagement

**Role:** OFF. Permanently killed.

**Rationale:** Does not align with audience or brand perception. B2B founder audience is not on TikTok for business decisions. Effort is better spent on LinkedIn and YouTube.

## The Discretion Principle (Cross-Platform)

This applies to ALL platforms:

- Clients don't want the world to know who runs their brand operations
- StartSuite's public content is for founders/operators/investors, NOT end consumers of client products
- Show how StartSuite thinks, not a portfolio reel
- Never reveal proprietary client processes or internal details without explicit consent
- Collab/tag model on Instagram respects client discretion
- Some clients prefer not to be featured publicly — always respect this

---

## Content Quality Gate (Cross-Platform)

Before any content goes live on any platform, it must pass:

1. **Expert insight test:** Does it tell the audience something they didn't already know?
2. **Grabber test:** Does the opening demand a reaction or make someone rewind?
3. **Platform-native test:** Is the format and pacing right for this specific platform?
4. **Discretion test:** Does it respect client confidentiality?
5. **Brand voice test:** Is it confident, direct, strategic, and data-backed?
