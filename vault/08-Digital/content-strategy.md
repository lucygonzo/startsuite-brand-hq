# Content Strategy

**Last Updated:** March 26, 2026
**Source:** brandData.ts digital.contentStrategy, project_content_strategy.md (Aaron meetings, March 2026)

---

## Key Takeaway

StartSuite's digital strategy is focused: LinkedIn is primary, YouTube is long-form authority, Instagram is presence only (no active strategy), TikTok is OFF. The monthly shoot day model (war room + expert guest) creates the content pipeline. Every piece of content must pass the expert insight standard. The discretion principle protects proprietary processes and client details.

---

## Content Pillars

1. **Brand strategy education** — expert-level insights on positioning, competitive intelligence, and brand architecture
2. **Behind-the-scenes of the creative operating system** — how Brand HQ works, competitive research process, AI tools in action
3. **Client transformation stories** — how companies went from brand chaos to brand system (with discretion for private clients)
4. **AI-empowered creative insights** — how senior judgment + AI acceleration produces better outcomes than either alone
5. **Monthly shoot day content** — war room internal discussions + external expert guest interviews

---

## Content Formats

- LinkedIn posts (thought leadership, 3x/week, Reagan + Lucy personal brands)
- YouTube long-form (educational, monthly from shoot day material)
- Monthly shoot day (war room internal + expert guest interview)
- Case studies (when published)
- Brand teardowns (competitor analysis as content)

---

## Monthly Shoot Day Model

The monthly shoot day is the content production engine that fuels all channels.

### First Half: Internal War Room
- Lucy + Reagan
- Low-fi, conversational, behind-the-scenes
- Shows how StartSuite actually thinks
- Raw, authentic format

### Second Half: Structured Podcast
- External guest (founder, investor, VC partner, client)
- 2-hour recorded conversation
- Structured format with prepared topics

### Output
One shoot day produces enough raw material for weeks of platform-native content through the hub-and-spoke model.

---

## Content Quality Standard (Expert Insight Test)

Every piece of content must pass this test before publishing:

- **"Grabber" not "hook"** — demands a reaction, makes someone rewind
- **Expert insight test:** Does it tell the audience something they didn't already know?
- **Hub-and-spoke model:** Long-form becomes 3-5 short clips, quote graphics, carousels
- **Platform-specific editing:** LinkedIn content is different from Instagram content is different from YouTube content

---

## Hub-and-Spoke Distribution

```
Long-Form Content (YouTube / Shoot Day)
    |
    ├── 3-5 short clips (platform-native)
    ├── Quote graphics
    ├── Carousels (LinkedIn)
    ├── Teaser copy for LinkedIn (drive "see more" click)
    └── Repurposed snippets for Instagram
```

---

## The Discretion Principle

- Clients don't want the world to know who runs their brand operations
- StartSuite's public content is for founders/operators/investors, NOT end consumers of client products
- Show how StartSuite thinks, not a portfolio reel
- Collab/tag model on Instagram respects client discretion
- Some clients prefer not to be featured publicly — this is always respected
