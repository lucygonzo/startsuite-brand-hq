# Chief — Competitive Profile

> Category: Speaking to Our People
> Last researched: March 26, 2026
> Research depth: Attention
> Relevance to StartSuite: Medium

---

## Overview

Chief is a private network for senior women executives, offering peer advisory groups (Core Groups), executive coaching, and curated networking. Founded in 2019, Chief grew rapidly during the remote-work era as senior women leaders sought structured peer connections. The membership is selective — applicants must hold VP-level or above titles — and the community model centers on small-group peer advisory (8-10 executives per Core Group) facilitated by executive coaches.

Chief has faced headwinds as the market corrected, with reports of layoffs and membership retention challenges. However, the model — peer community as the product, with content and events as supporting features — remains relevant.

## Why They Matter to StartSuite

StartSuite's Persona 03 (marketing director, typically female, mid-career) is the Chief archetype. She's a senior professional who values peer community, invests in her development, and makes purchasing decisions for creative services. Understanding how Chief recruits, retains, and engages this persona reveals what she expects from membership-style offerings. The retention dynamics are especially instructive: what makes a senior professional renew a $1,100/yr membership year after year?

## Key Numbers

- Membership: $1,100/yr (reported)
- Model: Selective application, peer advisory groups
- Core feature: Small-group peer advisory (Core Groups)
- Target: VP+ level women executives
- Challenges: Retention, post-pandemic engagement

## What StartSuite Can Learn

1. **Peer connection beats content.** Chief's most valued feature isn't the speaker series or the content library — it's the Core Group. Senior professionals stay for relationships, not information. If StartSuite builds community elements, the highest-value component will be small-group peer connections between marketing leaders or founders, not webinars or content.
2. **Selectivity creates value.** Chief's application process isn't just filtering — it's marketing. Being "accepted" into Chief signals status. StartSuite's higher tiers (Enterprise, Dream Client) could benefit from selectivity: "We work with 50 companies at this tier" creates scarcity and status.
3. **Retention is the hardest problem.** Chief's reported struggles with retention show that even strong community models face churn when the initial excitement fades. StartSuite's monthly subscription model needs built-in retention mechanics — evolving brand context, regular deliverables, ongoing strategy — that give members a reason to stay beyond the first engagement.

## Positioning & Language Worth Noting

- "A private network designed for the most powerful women in business" — exclusivity and aspiration
- The word "private" does heavy lifting — it implies value that's hidden from non-members
- Chief sells belonging, not access — the emotional value of being "in the room" with peers
- Cautionary note: exclusivity can limit scale. StartSuite's tier model needs to feel selective at the top without being exclusionary at the bottom
