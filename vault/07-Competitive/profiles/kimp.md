# Kimp — Competitive Profile

> Category: Direct Competitor
> Last researched: March 26, 2026
> Threat level: Low
> Primary tier impact: Day-Ones
> Sources: kimp.io

---

## Company Overview

- Founded ~2019
- Team size undisclosed; distributed designer and video editor network
- No public funding or revenue data
- Positioned as affordable design + video subscription

## Product & Pricing

- Unlimited design and/or video subscription
- Graphics: $599-699/mo — unlimited graphic design requests
- Video: $699-799/mo — unlimited video editing requests
- Graphics + Video: $995-1,195/mo — both services combined
- Promo pricing: First 2 months at $349.50
- 3 concurrent requests on Graphics tier
- 24hr-4day turnaround depending on complexity
- Unlimited revisions included

## Target Audience & GTM

- SMBs needing consistent design and video output on a budget
- Marketing teams producing social content, ads, and basic video
- Content marketing acquisition (blog, how-to guides)
- Promo pricing hooks ($349.50 first 2 months) for trial conversion
- Positions against hiring in-house or using freelancers

## How They Position

- Affordable, predictable creative output — design AND video in one subscription
- Video inclusion differentiates from pure design subscriptions
- Emphasis on volume (unlimited requests, unlimited revisions)
- Key claim: get a full design + video team for under $1,200/mo

## Threat Assessment

- **Threat level:** Low
- **Tiers impacted:** Day-Ones who are purely price-shopping for design output
- **Their strongest argument:** Design + video bundled at under $1,200/mo. The video add-on differentiates from Penji/ManyPixels.
- **StartSuite's strongest argument:** Operating system vs output factory. Kimp produces files; StartSuite builds brand intelligence. No strategy, no context, no compounding value.
- **Trending toward our positioning?** No — firmly commodity design/video subscription
- **Biggest vulnerability:** Completely interchangeable with Penji, ManyPixels, and other budget design subscriptions. No differentiation beyond video bundling. Zero brand intelligence. No switching costs.

## What StartSuite Can Learn

- Video bundling is smart packaging — consider whether StartSuite should bundle video editing/motion into Foundations as standard or add-on.
- The $349.50 intro pricing creates a low-friction entry point. StartSuite's Brand Discovery already serves this function but at higher price — make sure the value differential is crystal clear.
- Their pricing spans a tight range ($599-$1,195) which simplifies the decision. Keep StartSuite's pricing architecture equally legible.

## Key Quotes / Copy to Watch

- Track intro pricing changes — aggressive discounting signals competitive pressure
- Watch if they add AI features to compete with Canva/Gamma
- Video positioning language — if StartSuite adds video, study how Kimp frames it
