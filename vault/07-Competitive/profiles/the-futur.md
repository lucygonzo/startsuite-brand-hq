# The Futur — Competitive Profile

> Category: Speaking to Our People
> Last researched: March 26, 2026
> Research depth: Attention
> Relevance to StartSuite: Medium

---

## Overview

The Futur is a creative business education company founded by Chris Do, a former Emmy-winning motion designer and Art Center faculty member. The brand combines YouTube content (2M+ subscribers), paid courses ($500-$2,000+), coaching programs, and live workshops — all focused on teaching creative professionals how to run profitable businesses. Topics include pricing, sales, brand strategy, client management, and creative leadership.

Chris Do's personal brand IS The Futur. He teaches on camera, hosts conversations, and models the confident creative businessperson archetype. The content style is direct, sometimes confrontational, always opinionated — and it works, particularly with designers and creative directors who feel undervalued.

## Why They Matter to StartSuite

The Futur's audience — creative professionals who want to build real businesses around their craft — overlaps significantly with StartSuite's potential referral network and even its team. The content strategy is a direct parallel: The Futur teaches creative business education on YouTube; StartSuite could teach brand strategy and creative systems thinking. More importantly, The Futur demonstrates how a creative education brand can charge premium prices by building authority through free content first.

## Key Numbers

- YouTube: 2M+ subscribers
- Courses: $500-$2,000+
- Coaching: Premium group and 1:1 programs
- Model: Free content (YouTube) → paid courses → high-ticket coaching

## What StartSuite Can Learn

1. **Free content builds authority for paid offers.** Chris Do gives away 95% of his thinking on YouTube for free. The 5% that's paid is the structured application — frameworks, templates, live coaching. StartSuite should consider a content strategy where strategic brand thinking is free (blog, YouTube, social) and the structured application (Brand Discovery, Foundations) is paid.
2. **Personal brand amplifies company brand.** The Futur wouldn't exist without Chris Do's personal brand. StartSuite's founder(s) should consider how their personal authority — speaking, writing, teaching — creates credibility for the company brand. The creative industry trusts people before companies.
3. **Teach the philosophy, sell the system.** The Futur teaches "why you should charge more" for free and sells "how to charge more" as a course. StartSuite could teach "why brand context matters" for free and sell the system that delivers it.
4. **Opinioned content outperforms neutral content.** Chris Do's most viral content is his most opinionated — pricing debates, value-based design arguments, calling out industry practices. StartSuite's content should take strong positions on brand strategy, not just share tips.

## Positioning & Language Worth Noting

- "An online education platform that teaches the business of design" — clear, functional
- Chris Do frequently says "charge what you're worth" — a rallying cry that positions him as an ally to undercharging creatives
- The Futur proves that YouTube can be a primary channel for premium B2B offerings, not just consumer brands
- Content style: long-form conversations, role-plays, live coaching — formats that build trust and demonstrate expertise
