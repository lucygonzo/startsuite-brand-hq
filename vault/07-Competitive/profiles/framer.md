# Framer — Competitive Profile

> Category: Adjacent Competitor
> Last researched: March 26, 2026
> Threat level: Low
> Primary tier impact: Scalers, Operators
> Sources: framer.com

---

## Company Overview

- Website builder and design tool for marketing websites
- Popular with designers, agencies, and startups
- Significant community and template marketplace
- October 2025: Major pricing overhaul — simplified from 6 tiers to 5
- Strong brand within the design/startup community

## Product & Pricing

- No-code website builder with designer-grade control
- Free: Basic features, Framer subdomain
- Basic: $10/mo — custom domain, basic site
- Pro: $30/mo — CMS, advanced features, analytics
- Scale: $100/mo — high traffic, multiple editors, advanced CMS
- Enterprise: Custom pricing — SSO, dedicated support, SLAs
- Template marketplace for quick starts
- Localization, AI features, and CMS built in

## Target Audience & GTM

- Designers building marketing websites (primary)
- Agencies creating client websites
- Startups that want polished marketing sites without developers
- Community-driven growth: templates, tutorials, designer community
- SEO: "no-code website builder" and related keywords
- Template marketplace drives both acquisition and revenue

## How They Position

- Designer-grade website builder — more design control than Webflow, more polished than Squarespace
- Speed: ship marketing websites fast without engineering
- Positions against Webflow (too complex), Squarespace (too rigid), WordPress (too legacy)
- Key claim: publish beautiful, high-performance marketing websites without code

## Threat Assessment

- **Threat level:** Low
- **Tiers impacted:** Scalers and Operators who need marketing websites — but Framer is a tool, not a service competitor
- **Their strongest argument:** Best-in-class no-code website builder for designers. If a StartSuite member wants to build their own website, Framer is likely the best option.
- **StartSuite's strongest argument:** Framer is a website builder, not a brand management system. It builds one asset (website); StartSuite manages the entire brand intelligence layer. No strategy, no competitive monitoring, no brand intelligence.
- **Trending toward our positioning?** No — firmly a website builder tool
- **Biggest vulnerability:** Website-only tool. No strategy, no brand management, no intelligence. Commoditized space with Webflow, Squarespace, and WordPress alternatives.

## What StartSuite Can Learn

- **Framer is a potential tool in StartSuite's stack, not a competitor.** StartSuite could use Framer to build client websites as part of Dream Client engagements. Consider listing it as a recommended tool.
- **Their pricing simplification (6 tiers to 5) signals maturity.** Complex pricing confuses buyers. StartSuite should keep its pricing architecture clean and legible.
- **The designer community is a distribution channel.** Framer's community of designers could include future StartSuite collaborators or referral partners.
- **Template marketplace is a revenue and acquisition engine.** If StartSuite ever offers brand templates, study Framer's marketplace model.

## Key Quotes / Copy to Watch

- Track pricing changes — their October 2025 overhaul suggests ongoing optimization
- Watch AI feature development in their website builder
- Monitor if they expand beyond websites into broader brand tools
- Track agency/team features that might create service-layer overlap
