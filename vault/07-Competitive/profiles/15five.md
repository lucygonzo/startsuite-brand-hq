## Overview

15Five is a performance management platform built around the concept of continuous check-ins. The name comes from the core mechanic: employees spend 15 minutes writing a weekly report, managers spend 5 minutes reading and responding. The platform has expanded to include OKRs, engagement surveys, performance reviews, and 1-on-1 agendas, but the weekly check-in rhythm remains the core differentiator. Pricing runs $4-$14/user/mo across tiers.

The model transforms a periodic activity (annual reviews) into an ongoing intelligence feed (weekly check-ins). This produces richer data, earlier problem detection, and stronger manager-employee relationships.

## Why They Matter to StartSuite

15Five's weekly check-in model is a structural parallel to StartSuite's ongoing brand monitoring and reporting. Both transform what was traditionally periodic (annual brand audit / annual performance review) into continuous intelligence (monthly brand reports / weekly check-ins). The lesson is in how ongoing, lightweight reporting creates stickier relationships and better outcomes than periodic deep dives.

## Key Numbers

- Pricing: $4-$14/user/mo (tiered)
- Core mechanic: 15-minute weekly check-in
- Products: Check-ins, OKRs, reviews, engagement, 1-on-1s
- Model: Ongoing intelligence vs. periodic assessment

## What StartSuite Can Learn

1. **Continuous beats periodic.** 15Five proved that weekly lightweight check-ins produce better outcomes than quarterly deep reviews. StartSuite's monthly brand reports should follow the same principle — continuous, lightweight, actionable intelligence is more valuable than an annual brand audit.
2. **The ritual creates the retention.** 15Five's weekly cadence becomes a habit. Employees and managers build it into their routine. StartSuite's monthly brand reviews, creative check-ins, and asset deliverables should become similarly habitual — when clients expect the rhythm, they don't cancel.
3. **Data over time is the moat.** 15Five's engagement data over 52 weeks tells a story that no single survey can. StartSuite's brand context over 12+ months of engagement tells a story that no single brand audit can. The accumulation of longitudinal data creates unique value.
4. **Lightweight inputs, rich outputs.** 15Five asks for 15 minutes of input and produces insights on engagement, performance, and risk. StartSuite should aim for similar efficiency: lightweight client inputs (brief approvals, preference signals) that feed rich outputs (brand reports, strategic recommendations).

## Positioning & Language Worth Noting

- "Strategic performance management" — not "review software" but "performance management," elevating the category
- The name itself (15Five) IS the value proposition — 15 minutes in, 5 minutes out
- The shift from "annual review" to "continuous performance" mirrors StartSuite's shift from "periodic rebrand" to "continuous creative operating system"
