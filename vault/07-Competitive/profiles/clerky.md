# Clerky — Competitive Profile

> Category: Same Industry, Different Problem
> Last researched: March 26, 2026
> Research depth: Light
> Relevance to StartSuite: Medium

---

## Overview

Clerky is a YC-backed company that automates startup legal paperwork — incorporation, fundraising documents, hiring agreements, and equity. It is a self-serve platform designed for pre-seed and seed-stage founders who need legal documents but cannot afford (or don't yet need) a startup attorney. Clerky has become a quiet default in the YC ecosystem, handling legal formation for thousands of companies.

Their model is transactional — founders pay per document package rather than a monthly subscription. This makes Clerky a point-of-need tool rather than an ongoing relationship, which limits recurring revenue but maximizes adoption at the earliest company stage.

## Why They Matter to StartSuite

Clerky serves founders at the exact moment they're forming a company — the same moment they need a brand. A founder who incorporates on Clerky on Monday needs a logo and brand foundation by Friday. The partnership potential is medium: Clerky could recommend StartSuite as a next step after incorporation, but the relationship is transactional rather than ongoing. The real lesson is in how Clerky embedded itself in the YC onboarding flow.

## Key Numbers

- Backed by: Y Combinator
- Pricing: Transactional (per document package)
- Focus: Pre-seed and seed-stage startups
- Key channel: YC ecosystem

## What StartSuite Can Learn

1. **Ecosystem embedding beats marketing.** Clerky doesn't advertise. They're recommended by YC partners, startup attorneys, and accelerators. The product spreads through trusted recommendation, not campaigns. StartSuite should pursue the same — get recommended by the people founders already trust.
2. **Self-serve for early stage, human for later.** Clerky automates what can be automated and sends founders to attorneys for complex matters. This mirrors StartSuite's model: Brand Discovery is the self-serve(ish) wedge, Foundations and Dream Client add human expertise as complexity grows.

## Positioning & Language Worth Noting

- "Legal paperwork for startups" — no jargon, no overselling
- They position legal docs as infrastructure, not a creative service — same reframing StartSuite applies to brand
- The YC stamp of approval IS the marketing — credibility by association
