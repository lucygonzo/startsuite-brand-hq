# Fractional CMOs (Chief Outsiders model) — Competitive Profile

> Category: Similar Business Model
> Last researched: March 26, 2026
> Research depth: Model
> Relevance to StartSuite: High

---

## Overview

The fractional CMO market provides companies with part-time, senior marketing leadership without the commitment of a full-time C-suite hire. Chief Outsiders is the largest organized network, with 125+ fractional CMOs available for engagements ranging from $1,500-$30,000/mo (or $300-$500/hr). Other players include CMOx, Authentic Brand, and independent fractional CMOs.

The model serves the gap between "we need marketing strategy" and "we can't afford a $350K/yr CMO." Typical clients are companies at $5M-$50M revenue — the Established and Enterprise tiers in StartSuite's framework. Fractional CMOs usually work 10-20 hours/week, attend leadership meetings, set marketing strategy, and manage agencies and teams.

## Why They Matter to StartSuite

Fractional CMOs compete directly with StartSuite for budget at the Established and Enterprise tiers. When a company has $5K-$15K/mo for marketing leadership, they choose between a fractional CMO and a creative operating system like StartSuite. The offerings are complementary in theory (fractional CMO sets strategy, StartSuite executes brand) but competitive in practice (limited budget, overlapping scope). Understanding fractional CMO pricing, delivery, and limitations reveals how StartSuite should position against and alongside this model.

## Key Numbers

- Pricing: $1,500-$30,000/mo (typically $7K-$15K/mo)
- Hourly: $300-$500/hr
- Chief Outsiders network: 125+ CMOs
- Target: Companies at $5M-$50M revenue
- Engagement: 10-20 hrs/week, 6-12 month typical

## What StartSuite Can Learn

1. **The budget line is the same.** A company paying $10K/mo for a fractional CMO could pay $3K/mo for StartSuite Foundations + $7K for execution — or vice versa. StartSuite must either replace the fractional CMO (provide strategic guidance + execution) or partner with them (be the execution layer the fractional CMO directs). The "and" positioning is stronger than "or."
2. **Senior talent doesn't scale.** Chief Outsiders' 125+ CMOs is impressive, but each CMO can only serve 2-3 clients. StartSuite's model scales better if technology amplifies the creative director's capacity. The goal: one StartSuite creative director serves more clients than one fractional CMO, because the context system does the heavy lifting.
3. **Knowledge walks out the door.** When a fractional CMO engagement ends, the strategy knowledge leaves with the person. This is StartSuite's biggest competitive advantage: the context vault retains everything. A company that leaves a fractional CMO loses institutional memory. A company on StartSuite retains every brand decision, asset, and strategic rationale in the system.
4. **Position as the system, not the person.** Fractional CMOs sell personal expertise. StartSuite sells a system that includes expertise. The system is more scalable, more consistent, and more retainable. Companies should see StartSuite as the creative infrastructure that persists regardless of which creative director is assigned.

## Positioning & Language Worth Noting

- Chief Outsiders: "Growth strategy and execution for mid-market companies" — results-oriented, not credential-oriented
- Fractional CMOs sell "strategic marketing leadership" — StartSuite could sell "strategic creative leadership" as a parallel
- The word "fractional" itself implies compromise (you get a fraction of a CMO). StartSuite should position as "complete" — a full creative operating system, not a fraction of an agency
- Key vulnerability: fractional CMO quality varies wildly because it depends entirely on the individual. StartSuite's systemized approach offers more consistency
