## Overview

Ignition (formerly Practice Ignition) is a client engagement platform for professional services firms — primarily accounting, bookkeeping, and financial advisory. The platform handles proposals, engagement letters, billing, and payments in one workflow. When a client signs an Ignition proposal, it automatically sets up recurring billing, connects to the firm's accounting software, and initiates the onboarding workflow.

Ignition represents the "productization of professional services" movement — turning bespoke, relationship-dependent service businesses into systematized, technology-enabled operations. They serve thousands of firms and have processed billions in client billings.

## Why They Matter to StartSuite

Ignition solved the problem of productizing professional services — exactly what StartSuite is doing with creative services. Accounting firms using Ignition went from custom proposals and manual invoicing to standardized engagements with automated billing. StartSuite is taking creative services from custom project scoping and manual delivery to standardized tiers with systematized delivery. The challenges are identical: how do you make a relationship-dependent service feel productized without losing the human touch?

## What StartSuite Can Learn

1. **Productization starts with the engagement, not the delivery.** Ignition doesn't change how accountants do bookkeeping — it changes how they sell, scope, and bill bookkeeping. StartSuite's productization should similarly focus on the engagement layer: standardized tiers, clear scoping, automated onboarding, predictable billing. The creative work itself can remain human and bespoke.
2. **Automated billing reduces churn.** Ignition's automatic recurring billing means clients don't make a monthly decision to continue — they opted into ongoing engagement at signing. StartSuite's subscription model should minimize friction at renewal: automatic monthly billing, not monthly invoice approval.
3. **The proposal IS the product demo.** Ignition's proposals show the client exactly what they're getting — scope, pricing, timeline — in a polished, interactive format. StartSuite's Brand Discovery proposal should function the same way: a preview of what the systematic, premium experience feels like. The sales process should embody the product.
4. **Professional services want to be SaaS.** Ignition exists because professional services firms want the predictability and scalability of SaaS. StartSuite IS the SaaS-ification of creative services. The market trend is toward productized services across every professional category.

## Positioning & Language Worth Noting

- "The leading revenue automation platform for professional services" — "revenue automation" reframes billing as something strategic
- Ignition positions itself as infrastructure for the service business, not a tool for the service delivery — same framing StartSuite should use
- The rebrand from "Practice Ignition" to "Ignition" signaled broader ambition beyond accounting — naming lesson for StartSuite as it expands beyond startups
