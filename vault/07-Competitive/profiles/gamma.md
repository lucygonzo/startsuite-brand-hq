# Gamma — Competitive Profile

> Category: Adjacent Competitor (MAJOR THREAT)
> Last researched: March 26, 2026
> Threat level: High
> Primary tier impact: Day-Ones, Scalers
> Sources: gamma.app

---

## Company Overview

- Founded ~2021
- Funding: $80M total; $68M Series B (November 2025) at $2.1B valuation
- Revenue: $100M+ ARR — PROFITABLE
- Team: 52 employees (extraordinary efficiency — ~$2M ARR per employee)
- Users: 70M globally, 1M+ pieces of content created daily
- One of the fastest-growing AI content platforms in the world

## Product & Pricing

- AI-powered presentations, websites, and documents
- Free: 400 AI credits
- Plus: $8/mo — basic AI features, limited credits
- Pro: $18/mo — full AI features, more credits, custom branding
- Ultra: $100/mo — maximum credits, priority support, advanced features
- Gamma 3.0 features "Gamma Agent" — researches topics, refines content, restyles designs
- Credit-based usage model layered on subscription tiers

## Target Audience & GTM

- Anyone creating presentations, pitch decks, proposals, or documents
- Founders building investor decks (direct overlap with StartSuite Day-Ones)
- Marketing teams creating internal and external presentations
- Product-led growth: free tier drives massive adoption
- Viral loop: shared presentations drive awareness
- SEO + organic: "AI presentation maker" keyword dominance

## How They Position

- AI-first content creation — presentations, docs, and websites from prompts
- Positions against PowerPoint (slow, manual), Google Slides (limited), Canva (template-bound)
- "Gamma Agent" positions as an AI collaborator, not just a tool
- Key claim: create polished presentations in minutes, not hours

## Threat Assessment

- **Threat level:** HIGH
- **Tiers impacted:** Day-Ones (pitch decks are their sweet spot) and Scalers (marketing presentations, proposals)
- **Their strongest argument:** $100M+ ARR at $18/mo proves massive demand for AI presentation tools. 70M users. Profitable with 52 people. The pitch deck use case — StartSuite's Day-Ones bread and butter — can be done in Gamma for $18/mo vs $2,500+ for Brand Discovery.
- **StartSuite's strongest argument:** Gamma output is generic. No brand context — it doesn't know your competitive landscape, your investor targets, your positioning strategy. Brand intelligence makes output contextual; Gamma output is one-size-fits-all. A Gamma deck looks like every other Gamma deck. An investor sees dozens of them.
- **Trending toward our positioning?** Partially — "Gamma Agent" adds research and refinement, but it's AI-only with no human strategy or brand intelligence layer
- **Biggest vulnerability:** No brand context. No competitive intelligence. No human strategy. No investor perspective. Generic AI output that looks like every other Gamma deck. The 100x price difference is their moat AND their ceiling — they can never add the strategy layer that justifies premium pricing.

## What StartSuite Can Learn

- **The price gap is the main objection.** $18/mo vs $3,000/mo is a 167x difference. StartSuite must make the value of brand intelligence tangible and undeniable. Show before/after: Gamma deck vs StartSuite deck, and the fundraising outcomes.
- **52 employees at $100M ARR is aspirational efficiency.** Study their product-led growth engine. StartSuite won't be PLG, but the lesson about leverage matters.
- **Pitch deck is a gateway drug.** Gamma captures users at pitch deck creation; StartSuite should capture them when the Gamma deck doesn't close the round. Build a "upgrade from Gamma" conversion narrative.
- **The "Gamma Agent" feature signals where AI content creation is headed.** Research + refine + restyle is the workflow. StartSuite's AI tools should follow this pattern but with brand context injected at every step.
- **Gamma proves the market for AI content creation is enormous.** 70M users want this. StartSuite's opportunity is the premium layer on top.

## Key Quotes / Copy to Watch

- "Gamma Agent" feature evolution — track capabilities quarterly
- Watch for brand context or brand kit features — signals move toward StartSuite's territory
- Track enterprise features and pricing — signals move upmarket
- Monitor investor deck templates and use cases specifically
- Watch user sentiment: "all Gamma decks look the same" is the complaint that drives StartSuite conversions
