# Lattice — Competitive Profile

> Category: Same Industry, Different Problem
> Last researched: March 26, 2026
> Research depth: Light
> Relevance to StartSuite: Medium

---

## Overview

Lattice is a people management platform that combines performance reviews, engagement surveys, OKRs, compensation management, and career development tools. They reached a $3B valuation by serving growth-stage companies (100-5,000 employees) — the exact stage where informal processes break and companies need systems. Lattice positions itself as the platform that helps companies build a high-performance culture through structured people practices.

Their sweet spot is the transition from "we wing it" to "we have a system" — companies that have outgrown spreadsheets for reviews and goal-tracking but aren't big enough for Workday.

## Why They Matter to StartSuite

Lattice proves there's a massive market in the transition from ad hoc to systematic at growth stage — the same transition StartSuite serves for brand. When a company adopts Lattice for people management, they're signaling operational maturity. That same company likely needs to systematize brand management. Lattice's GTM targeting (companies at the "we need a system now" inflection point) is a model for StartSuite's positioning.

## Key Numbers

- Valuation: $3B
- Target: Growth-stage companies (100-5,000 employees)
- Products: Performance, engagement, OKRs, comp, career
- GTM: Sales-led with strong content marketing

## What StartSuite Can Learn

1. **Target the inflection point.** Lattice doesn't sell to 5-person startups or 50,000-employee enterprises. They target the moment companies outgrow ad hoc processes. StartSuite should target the same moment for brand: when the founder realizes they can't manage the brand on Canva and vibes anymore.
2. **Bundle related problems.** Lattice started with performance reviews and added engagement, OKRs, comp, and career. Each addition made the platform stickier and the data more valuable. StartSuite's progression from Brand Discovery to Foundations to Add-ons follows the same bundling logic.
3. **Culture as positioning.** Lattice doesn't sell software — they sell "high-performance culture." The tool is the vehicle, the outcome is the pitch. StartSuite shouldn't sell brand assets — it should sell "creative clarity" or "brand confidence."

## Positioning & Language Worth Noting

- "People management platform" — they own the category name
- "Make work meaningful" — aspiration over function
- They sell to HR but position for the CEO — same dual-audience challenge StartSuite faces (sell to marketing, position for the founder)
