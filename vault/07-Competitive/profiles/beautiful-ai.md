# Beautiful.ai — Competitive Profile

> Category: Adjacent Competitor
> Last researched: March 26, 2026
> Threat level: Medium
> Primary tier impact: Day-Ones, Scalers
> Sources: beautiful.ai

---

## Company Overview

- AI-powered presentation software
- Founded in San Francisco
- Backed by venture funding (undisclosed total)
- Positioned between Canva/Gamma (self-serve) and full agency work

## Product & Pricing

- AI presentation design with brand controls and analytics
- Pro: $12/mo (annual billing) — individual use
- Team: $40-50/user/mo — collaboration, shared brand, analytics
- Enterprise: Custom pricing — SSO, admin controls, dedicated support
- One-time option: $45 per presentation (no subscription required)
- 14-day free trial
- Slide analytics: track who views and engages with presentations

## Target Audience & GTM

- Sales teams (slide analytics for tracking prospect engagement)
- Marketing teams creating branded presentations
- Founders building pitch decks
- Product-led growth with free trial conversion
- The $45/presentation one-time option captures users who don't want subscriptions

## How They Position

- AI handles design decisions so you focus on content
- Brand control built in — consistent output without design skills
- Slide analytics differentiate from Gamma/Canva (know who viewed your deck)
- Positions against manual slide design and generic templates
- Key claim: AI-designed slides that stay on brand with engagement tracking

## Threat Assessment

- **Threat level:** Medium
- **Tiers impacted:** Day-Ones (pitch decks) and Scalers (sales/marketing presentations)
- **Their strongest argument:** Slide analytics are genuinely useful for sales teams. Brand controls at $12/mo are accessible. The one-time $45 option captures price-sensitive users.
- **StartSuite's strongest argument:** Presentation-only tool with no broader brand management. No strategy, no competitive intelligence, no brand intelligence layer. Analytics tell you who opened the deck; they don't tell you if the deck tells the right story.
- **Trending toward our positioning?** No — firmly presentation-focused
- **Biggest vulnerability:** Presentation-only. Squeezed between Gamma (cheaper, AI-first) and Canva (broader platform). No strategy layer. Brand controls are surface-level (colors, fonts) not strategic (positioning, messaging, narrative).

## What StartSuite Can Learn

- **Slide analytics are a smart feature.** Knowing who viewed a pitch deck and which slides they spent time on is genuinely valuable for founders. Consider whether StartSuite should offer deck analytics as a Dream Client add-on.
- **The $45 one-time option is clever.** It captures users who won't commit to a subscription. Could StartSuite offer a one-time pitch deck review or audit?
- **Brand controls at the presentation level are table stakes now.** StartSuite's brand management must go deeper than colors and fonts to differentiate.
- **They're squeezed between Gamma and Canva.** This is a cautionary tale — being "better than free but not as good as premium" is a dangerous position. StartSuite must stay firmly premium.

## Key Quotes / Copy to Watch

- Track slide analytics feature development — this is their differentiator
- Watch pricing changes, especially vs Gamma's aggressive pricing
- Monitor enterprise sales messaging and client logos
- Watch for broader brand management features beyond presentations
