## Overview

IDEO is the design and innovation consultancy that popularized "design thinking" as a business methodology. Founded in 1991, IDEO built its reputation on human-centered design for products (the first Apple mouse), services, and organizational strategy. Their influence extends far beyond client work — IDEO essentially created the "design thinking" category through books (Change by Design by Tim Brown), Stanford's d.school partnership, and relentless evangelism of their methodology.

IDEO has contracted significantly in recent years, with multiple rounds of layoffs as the consulting market shifted. But their brand legacy — making "design thinking" a mainstream business concept — remains one of the most successful category creation stories in professional services.

## Why They Matter to StartSuite

IDEO proved that a design firm can create a category — "design thinking" — and own it so completely that the methodology becomes synonymous with the firm. This is exactly what StartSuite needs to do with "creative operating system." IDEO didn't just do design work; they defined what design work meant for business. StartSuite shouldn't just deliver brand services; it should define what a creative operating system means for growing companies.

## Key Numbers

- Founded: 1991
- Peak: 700+ employees across global offices
- Category created: "Design thinking"
- Key publication: "Change by Design" by Tim Brown
- Recent trajectory: Significant contraction, layoffs

## What StartSuite Can Learn

1. **Category creation is the highest-leverage positioning.** IDEO didn't compete on "better design" — they created "design thinking" and made themselves the definitive source. StartSuite should invest in making "creative operating system" a recognized category: content, frameworks, language, and case studies that establish the concept, not just the company.
2. **Methodology is more scalable than taste.** IDEO's methodology (empathize, define, ideate, prototype, test) can be taught, licensed, and applied without IDEO being in the room. StartSuite's methodology — if codified clearly enough — could similarly scale beyond direct delivery. The methodology becomes the brand.
3. **Academic partnerships amplify credibility.** IDEO's partnership with Stanford's d.school gave design thinking academic credibility. StartSuite could partner with business programs, accelerators, or MBA programs to give "creative operating system" thinking institutional backing.
4. **CAUTIONARY: Category creation without commercial moat is fragile.** IDEO created design thinking, but anyone can practice it. The category escaped the firm's control. StartSuite's "creative operating system" needs to be tied to proprietary tools, context systems, and delivery models — not just a methodology that competitors can copy.

## Positioning & Language Worth Noting

- "We create positive impact through design" — purpose-driven, expansive
- IDEO positioned design as a strategic business tool, not an aesthetic discipline — same reframing StartSuite applies to brand
- The phrase "human-centered design" became IDEO's gift to the business lexicon — what phrase will StartSuite contribute?
- IDEO's contraction shows that category creation alone doesn't sustain a business — you need ongoing commercial relevance tied to the category you created
