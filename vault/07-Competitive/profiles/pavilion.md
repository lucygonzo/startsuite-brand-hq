# Pavilion — Competitive Profile

> Category: Speaking to Our People
> Last researched: March 26, 2026
> Research depth: Attention
> Relevance to StartSuite: High

---

## Overview

Pavilion (formerly Revenue Collective) is a professional community for go-to-market leaders — CROs, CMOs, VPs of Sales, and revenue executives. Founded by Sam Jacobs, Pavilion raised a $25M Series A and built a membership model tiered by seniority: Associate for individual contributors, Member for directors/VPs, and Executive for C-suite. Each tier includes peer communities, educational programs, job placement support, and live events.

The rebrand from Revenue Collective to Pavilion signaled ambition to expand beyond revenue leaders into a broader professional community. The tiered model by seniority is particularly relevant — different career stages get different communities, content, and pricing.

## Why They Matter to StartSuite

Pavilion's tiered membership by seniority mirrors StartSuite's tier model by company stage. The structural parallel is direct: Pavilion segments by career stage (IC → VP → C-suite), StartSuite segments by company stage (Startup → Growth → Enterprise). Pavilion's GTM leaders — CMOs, VPs of Marketing — are the decision-makers who buy creative services. They're literally StartSuite's buyer persona organized into a community. Understanding what Pavilion members value and how retention works across tiers reveals how StartSuite's tier model should function.

## Key Numbers

- Raised: $25M Series A
- Tiers: Associate / Member / Executive (priced by seniority)
- Focus: GTM leaders (CROs, CMOs, VPs of Sales/Marketing)
- Features: Peer community, education, job placement, events

## What StartSuite Can Learn

1. **Tier by stage, not just price.** Pavilion doesn't just charge more for higher tiers — the experience changes. Executive members get smaller groups, more exclusive events, and direct peer connections. StartSuite's tiers should similarly differentiate on experience, not just deliverables. Enterprise tier gets strategic partnership, not just more assets.
2. **The CMO community is a channel.** Pavilion members are CMOs and VPs of Marketing. These are the people who approve creative budgets. StartSuite should explore Pavilion-style community sponsorships or partnerships as a direct channel to decision-makers.
3. **Seniority segmentation prevents community dilution.** By separating ICs from executives, Pavilion ensures senior members get senior peers. StartSuite's tier model naturally achieves this — startup founders talk to startup founders, enterprise CMOs talk to enterprise CMOs (if community features are added).
4. **Job placement as retention.** Pavilion members stay partly because the network helps them find their next role. StartSuite's parallel: members stay partly because the brand context helps them perform better in their current role and makes transitions smoother.

## Positioning & Language Worth Noting

- "The community for go-to-market leaders" — specific audience ownership
- The rebrand from "Revenue Collective" to "Pavilion" was aspirational — broader, more prestigious-sounding
- Tiered naming (Associate/Member/Executive) carries implicit hierarchy that motivates upgrading — people want to be "Executive" tier
- The $25M raise signals investor confidence that professional communities can scale — relevant validation for StartSuite's membership model
