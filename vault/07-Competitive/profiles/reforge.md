# Reforge — Competitive Profile

> Category: Speaking to Our People
> Last researched: March 26, 2026
> Research depth: Attention
> Relevance to StartSuite: High

---

## Overview

Reforge is a professional membership for growth, marketing, and product leaders. Founded by Brian Balfour (former VP Growth at HubSpot) and Andrew Chen (now a16z GP), Reforge offers structured programs on growth strategy, retention, experimentation, and product management. The membership includes live cohort-based programs, an on-demand content library, a Slack community, and increasingly, AI-powered tools that apply Reforge frameworks to members' actual work.

The audience is mid-to-senior marketing directors, product managers, and growth leads at tech companies — exactly the personas who make brand investment decisions at StartSuite's target companies.

## Why They Matter to StartSuite

StartSuite's Persona 01 (founder) and Persona 03 (marketing director) are Reforge members or aspirational Reforge members. These are professionals who invest in their craft, believe in frameworks, and spend $2K+/yr on professional development. Understanding what Reforge members value — structured thinking, peer community, practical application — reveals what StartSuite's audience expects from a premium membership-adjacent offering. Reforge also demonstrates the ceiling for premium professional memberships and how to retain senior professionals year over year.

## Key Numbers

- Individual membership: $1,995/yr
- Team plans: $23,995/yr (30 seats)
- Community: Slack-based, program-specific cohorts
- Shift: Adding AI tools alongside traditional course content
- Founded by: Brian Balfour, Andrew Chen

## What StartSuite Can Learn

1. **Premium pricing requires tangible outcomes.** Reforge charges $2K/yr because members can point to specific career outcomes — promotions, better strategy execution, frameworks they use daily. StartSuite's Foundations at $3K/mo needs the same: members must be able to say "my brand is measurably better because of StartSuite." Tangible artifacts (brand books, campaign systems, reporting) are the proof.
2. **Community is the retention mechanism.** Reforge's Slack community keeps members engaged between programs. The peer-to-peer connections justify renewal even when members have consumed the content. StartSuite should consider how community (founder peer groups, creative directors sharing approaches) could drive retention beyond the deliverables themselves.
3. **AI as application, not replacement.** Reforge is adding AI tools that apply frameworks to members' work — not replacing the frameworks with AI, but making them more usable. StartSuite should follow this pattern: AI that applies brand context to creative decisions, not AI that generates brands from scratch.
4. **The cohort model creates urgency.** Reforge programs run on a schedule, creating enrollment windows and urgency. StartSuite's Brand Discovery already has natural urgency (you need a brand now), but Foundations renewals could benefit from cohort-style cadences — quarterly brand reviews, annual strategy refreshes.

## Positioning & Language Worth Noting

- "Where top operators go to grow" — exclusivity + aspiration
- They use "programs" not "courses" — implies rigor and selectivity
- The pricing signals quality: $2K/yr is expensive enough to filter for serious professionals but cheap enough that a marketing director can expense it
- Reforge never discounts — the price IS the positioning
- Their shift to AI tools alongside courses signals that pure content memberships are evolving — StartSuite should note this as a market trend
