# Superside — Competitive Profile

> Category: Direct Competitor
> Last researched: March 26, 2026
> Threat level: Medium
> Primary tier impact: Scalers, Operators, Flagships
> Sources: superside.com

---

## Company Overview

- Founded 2015 in Oslo, Norway; fully remote
- Team: 700+ globally distributed
- Revenue: Median annual contract ~$90K, range $17.5K-$880K/yr per client
- No public funding data; bootstrapped-to-profitable trajectory implied by scale

## Product & Pricing

- Subscription creative services — design, motion, video, web, AI-enhanced output
- Pricing: $10K-$100K+/mo depending on scope and volume
- $1K/mo platform access fee on top of service fees
- 1-year minimum commitment required
- Custom pricing only — no public tiers or self-serve checkout
- Volume-based model: more output = higher tier

## Target Audience & GTM

- Enterprise teams with high-volume, ongoing creative needs
- Clients: Amazon, Shopify, Puma, Salesforce
- Homepage headline: "Your creative team's creative team"
- Sales-led GTM: outbound enterprise sales, demos, custom proposals
- Content strategy: case studies, enterprise thought leadership, design ops content
- Positions as an extension of in-house teams, not a replacement

## How They Position

- "Your creative team's creative team" — augmentation, not replacement
- Positions against hiring in-house designers (cost savings, speed, scalability)
- Emphasizes volume throughput, global talent pool, 24/7 coverage
- Claims AI-enhanced workflows but human-delivered output
- Key claim: faster and cheaper than building an in-house creative team at scale

## Threat Assessment

- **Threat level:** Medium
- **Tiers impacted:** Scalers (who might consider outsourcing at scale), Operators (who have budget for enterprise tools), Flagships (direct target overlap)
- **Their strongest argument:** Proven at enterprise scale with brand-name clients; massive team means they can absorb any volume
- **StartSuite's strongest argument:** Senior-led strategy + execution vs junior-heavy volume shop. Context compounds over time. Investor perspective baked in. Accessible pricing ($3K/mo vs $10K+/mo minimum).
- **Trending toward our positioning?** No — they are moving deeper into enterprise volume, not toward brand intelligence or strategy
- **Biggest vulnerability:** Volume model relies on junior execution. No strategy layer. No brand intelligence or competitive monitoring. Clients get output, not insight. High minimums lock out the exact audience StartSuite serves.

## What StartSuite Can Learn

- Their "creative team's creative team" framing is effective — it reduces perceived risk by positioning as augmentation rather than replacement. StartSuite could adapt this for the membership pitch.
- The 1-year commitment model creates revenue predictability. Consider whether annual pricing tiers make sense for Foundations.
- Their enterprise case study library is deep and conversion-focused. StartSuite should build a comparable case study engine for the startup/growth tier.

## Key Quotes / Copy to Watch

- "Your creative team's creative team"
- Emphasis on "AI-enhanced" creative — watch how they frame AI as tool vs replacement
- Enterprise trust signals everywhere: logos, case studies, SOC2/security messaging
