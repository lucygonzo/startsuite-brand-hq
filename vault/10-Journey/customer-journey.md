# Customer Journey

**Last Updated:** March 26, 2026
**Source:** brandData.journey (brandData.ts)

---

## Key Takeaway

The StartSuite client journey starts with awareness through LinkedIn content and referrals, moves through Brand Discovery as the proof-of-value entry point, and retains through Foundations as the compounding intelligence subscription. The journey is designed to demonstrate value before asking for ongoing commitment.

---

## Stage 1: Awareness

**Description:** Prospect first encounters StartSuite -- through LinkedIn content, YouTube, referrals from the 46Capital network, or word of mouth from existing clients.

**Touchpoints:**
- LinkedIn thought leadership post from Reagan or Lucy (primary)
- YouTube educational content or shoot day episode
- Referral from 46Capital, accelerator, or existing client
- Accelerator program introduction
- Google search for startup brand strategy or brand operating system

**Content Needed:**
- LinkedIn content that passes the expert insight standard (3x/week, Reagan + Lucy)
- YouTube content from monthly shoot day (war room + expert guest)
- Social proof posts (client milestones, but respecting discretion for clients who don't want to be featured)
- 46Capital co-branded introductions

**Founder Mindset:** "I've heard of these people" or "Someone I trust recommended them."

**Friction:** StartSuite is not yet well-known outside the 46Capital ecosystem. Content cadence needs to build awareness consistently.

**Success Metric:** Website visits from referral and organic sources, LinkedIn engagement rate

---

## Stage 2: Consideration

**Description:** Prospect visits the website, reviews services, explores Brand HQ demos and case studies, and evaluates whether Brand Discovery is the right entry point.

**Touchpoints:**
- start-suite.com homepage and services page
- Brand HQ demo or walkthrough
- Published case studies (when available)
- LinkedIn profile review of Reagan and Lucy
- Referral conversation with existing client

**Content Needed:**
- Published case studies with problem, process, and outcome
- Brand HQ demo or walkthrough experience
- Clear Brand Discovery pricing and scope description
- Comparison content: creative operating system vs. agency vs. freelancer vs. AI platform

**Founder Mindset:** "This looks different from what I've seen before. But what does Brand Discovery actually include, and what does it cost?"

**Friction:** Brand Discovery pricing is not yet published. No case studies yet. Prospect has to book a call to get basic information.

**Success Metric:** Brand Discovery call bookings

---

## Stage 3: Decision

**Description:** Prospect receives a Brand Discovery proposal, has a strategy conversation, and decides to move forward with the engagement.

**Touchpoints:**
- Brand Discovery proposal from Reagan or Lucy
- Strategy conversation and needs assessment
- Scope of work and pricing presentation
- Contract signing

**Content Needed:**
- Clear proposal template for Brand Discovery
- ROI framing: what does a living Brand HQ do for business decisions?
- Reference clients willing to speak to prospects
- Pricing options clearly presented

**Founder Mindset:** "I'm convinced this is different. Now I need to justify the investment to myself (and maybe my co-founder or CEO)."

**Friction:** Budget objection and category confusion. "What is a creative operating system and why do I need one?" requires education.

**Success Metric:** Brand Discovery contracts signed

---

## Stage 4: Onboarding (Brand Discovery)

**Description:** The Brand Discovery process. Deep research, competitive analysis, audience mapping, and Brand HQ creation and delivery. 2-4 weeks.

**Touchpoints:**
- Deep discovery session and brand intake
- Competitive landscape research and analysis
- Audience segmentation and persona development
- Brand HQ build and delivery
- Strategy presentation and walkthrough

**Content Needed:**
- Brand Discovery intake questionnaire
- Progress updates during the research process
- Brand HQ walkthrough and training
- Strategy presentation deck

**Founder Mindset:** "This is thorough. They're learning my business, my competitors, and my audience. This is not just design."

**Friction:** Client engagement -- they need to invest time in the discovery process for it to be valuable. Async-first process reduces friction.

**Success Metric:** Brand Discovery completion, client satisfaction score, Foundations conversion rate

---

## Stage 5: Retention (Foundations)

**Description:** The Foundations subscription at $3K/month. Context management, competitive monitoring, monthly brand state-of-the-union, office hours, strategy sessions. Context compounds every month.

**Touchpoints:**
- Monthly brand state-of-the-union report
- Competitive monitoring and landscape updates
- Office hours and strategy sessions
- Brand HQ updates and evolution
- Decision audit log maintenance
- AI resource access and personalized brand tools

**Content Needed:**
- Monthly report template showing brand intelligence updates and competitive evolution
- Competitive landscape tracking dashboards
- Decision audit log with context and rationale
- Proactive recommendations based on accumulated context

**Founder Mindset:** "The system is getting smarter every month. They know my brand, my competitors, and my audience better than I do. I can't afford to lose this context."

**Friction:** Proving compound value -- clients need to see the accumulation of intelligence to understand why the subscription is worth it month after month. Context is the moat, but it needs to be visible.

**Success Metric:** Subscription tenure (target: 12+ months), NPS score, add-on attach rate

---

## Stage 6: Expansion and Advocacy

**Description:** Client adds services, refers other companies, and becomes a StartSuite advocate. The referral flywheel. Case study participation for willing clients.

**Touchpoints:**
- Add-on service discussions (content, paid ads, web, AI automation)
- Referral conversations with other founders or marketing leaders
- Case study participation (for clients who consent)
- LinkedIn co-marketing (testimonials, shared content)

**Content Needed:**
- Clear add-on service menu with pricing
- Easy referral mechanism -- who to contact, what to say, what's in it for them
- Case study template and process
- Co-marketing playbook for willing clients

**Founder Mindset:** "StartSuite is part of why our brand works. I tell every founder I know about them."

**Friction:** Referrals require a formal ask and incentive structure. Some clients don't want to be featured publicly -- the discretion principle must be respected.

**Success Metric:** Add-on revenue per client, referrals generated per client, case studies published
